## Overview

The Weekly Review is a cornerstone practice of the Getting Things Done (GTD) methodology. It's a dedicated time (typically 1-2 hours) to review all projects, commitments, actions, and systems to ensure nothing falls through the cracks.

## Why It's Essential

David Allen: "The Weekly Review is the most critical success factor of GTD."

Without regular review:
- Projects stall silently
- Commitments are forgotten
- System becomes untrusted
- Inbox overflows
- Mind stays cluttered

## The Weekly Review Checklist

### 1. Get Clear (30 min)
**Collect loose materials:**
- Process all inboxes to zero (email, physical, digital)
- Review notes from meetings
- Gather loose papers
- Empty mind of open loops

**Process notes:**
- Review notebook pages
- Extract action items
- File reference material

### 2. Get Current (30 min)
**Review previous calendar:**
- Last two weeks of calendar
- Extract action items from past events
- Note unfinished items

**Review upcoming calendar:**
- Next two weeks ahead
- Identify preparation needed
- Block time for upcoming projects

**Review waiting for:**
- What are you waiting on from others?
- Send follow-up reminders
- Update project status

**Review project lists:**
- Does each project have a next action?
- Are any projects complete?
- Any new projects to add?

**Review next action lists:**
- Mark completed items
- Delete obsolete actions
- Update based on new information

### 3. Get Creative (30 min)
**Review someday/maybe:**
- Anything ready to activate now?
- Delete items no longer interesting
- Add new ideas

**Review goals and higher horizons:**
- Are projects aligned with goals?
- Any new projects needed?
- Course corrections required?

**Be creative:**
- What new opportunities exist?
- What could make next week great?
- Any innovative approaches to current projects?

## Scheduling

**When:**
- Same time each week (consistency builds habit)
- Common: Friday afternoon or Sunday evening
- Block 90-120 minutes uninterrupted

**Where:**
- Quiet space with no interruptions
- All tools available (computer, notebooks, files)
- Comfortable environment

**Preparation:**
- Turn off notifications
- Close unnecessary apps
- Have coffee/tea ready
- Treat as important meeting with yourself

## Benefits

### Trust in System
- Know nothing is forgotten
- Mind can relax
- System feels current

### Proactive vs. Reactive
- Spot problems before they're urgent
- Prepare for upcoming challenges
- Make strategic decisions

### Stress Reduction
- Clear head at week's end
- Sleep better
- Start week with clarity

### Better Decisions
- See whole picture
- Align daily actions with goals
- Identify misalignments

## Common Obstacles

### "I don't have time"
**Reality**: Review creates time by preventing:
- Forgotten commitments
- Last-minute scrambles
- Misaligned efforts
- System breakdown

### "I'll do it when things slow down"
**Reality**: Things never slow down. Review most valuable when busy.

### "It takes too long"
**Reality**: 
- First few reviews: 2-3 hours
- Once system is clean: 60-90 minutes
- Time saved during week: 5+ hours

### "It's boring"
**Reality**: Make it pleasant:
- Nice location
- Good music
- Favorite beverage
- View it as self-care

## Tips for Success

1. **Same time every week**: Build habit
2. **Block calendar**: Treat as unmovable appointment
3. **Use checklist**: Don't rely on memory
4. **Start small**: 30 minutes better than skipping
5. **Celebrate**: Acknowledge the accomplishment
6. **Review the review**: What made it smooth or difficult?

## Variations

### Daily Review (5-10 min)
- Check calendar for tomorrow
- Review today's completions
- Plan tomorrow's priorities

### Monthly Review (2-3 hours)
- Review all areas of responsibility
- Assess goal progress
- Major course corrections
- Clear out completed projects

### Quarterly Review (4-6 hours)
- Strategic planning
- Goal setting
- Life balance assessment
- Major commitments review

## The Result

**With regular weekly reviews:**
- "Water-like mind" - calm, clear, responsive
- Trust your system completely
- Make better decisions
- Reduce stress
- Accomplish more of what matters
- Feel in control

**Without weekly reviews:**
- System breakdown
- Forgotten commitments
- Constant firefighting
- Loss of trust in system
- Return to mental overwhelm
