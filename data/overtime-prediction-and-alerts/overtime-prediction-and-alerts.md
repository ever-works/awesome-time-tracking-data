## Overview

Overtime prediction uses real-time time tracking data to forecast when employees will exceed standard hours, triggering alerts to managers before overtime is incurred, enabling proactive workload redistribution.

## How Prediction Works

### Data Analysis
- Current week hours worked
- Pace of time accumulation
- Scheduled remaining shifts
- Historical overtime patterns
- Project deadlines

### Threshold Monitoring
- 40-hour weekly threshold (FLSA)
- 8-hour daily overtime (some states)
- Consecutive day limits
- Custom company thresholds

### Alert Triggers
- 75% of overtime threshold reached
- Projected to exceed based on current pace
- Scheduled shifts would cause overtime
- Pattern change from normal hours

## Alert Recipients

- **Manager**: Redistribution decisions
- **HR**: Compliance monitoring
- **Employee**: Self-management opportunity
- **Payroll**: Budget impact awareness

## Response Options

### Prevent Overtime
- Redistribute work to other team members
- Adjust schedule for remaining week
- Delay non-urgent tasks
- Approve flex time

### Approve Overtime
- Business need justifies cost
- No alternative resources available
- Client commitment requires completion
- Document approval

## Benefits

### Cost Control
- Prevent unexpected overtime expenses
- Better labor budget management
- Proactive vs reactive staffing
- Predictable payroll costs

### Compliance
- Avoid unintentional violations
- Document oversight efforts
- Prevent employee burnout
- Support work-life balance

### Resource Optimization
- Identify understaffed projects
- Balance workload across team
- Inform hiring decisions
- Improve scheduling accuracy
