## Overview

Notion does not include native time tracking functionality, as the platform focuses primarily on documentation and knowledge management. However, users can add time tracking to Notion through third-party integrations that connect external time tracking tools with Notion databases.

## Popular Time Tracking Integrations

**Everhour** - Offers the most seamless Notion integration, embedding time tracking directly into Notion's UI with a browser extension that allows starting timers and logging time without leaving Notion pages.

**TrackingTime** - Provides Notion integration enabling users to track time on Notion tasks and databases, with time data syncing back to TrackingTime for reporting.

**Toggl Track** - Can be integrated with Notion through Zapier or browser extensions, allowing time tracking on Notion items.

**Clockify** - Connects with Notion databases through integrations, enabling time logging on Notion tasks.

## How Integrations Work

1. **Connect Notion Database** - Link your Notion workspace to the time tracking tool
2. **Map Tasks** - Identify which Notion databases contain trackable items
3. **Start Tracking** - Use browser extension or tool interface to track time on Notion tasks
4. **Sync Data** - Time entries sync back to the tracking tool for reporting
5. **Optional Database Updates** - Some integrations can write time data back to Notion properties

## Limitations

Since time tracking isn't native to Notion:
- Requires separate subscription to time tracking tool
- May need browser extensions for seamless workflow
- Time data primarily lives in external tool, not Notion
- Reporting happens in the time tracking app, not Notion
- Can't access time data in Notion formulas or rollups

## Workarounds for Native Tracking

Some teams build manual time tracking in Notion using:
- Duration properties for manual time entry
- Formulas to calculate total time
- Relations to connect time entries to projects
- Templates for daily time logs

However, this requires manual data entry and lacks timer functionality.

## Best Approach

For teams using Notion who need robust time tracking:
1. Choose Everhour for the most integrated Notion experience
2. Use TrackingTime if you need 60+ tool integrations beyond Notion
3. Select Toggl Track for simple time tracking with Notion task context

## Alternative: ClickUp

Teams requiring native time tracking within their all-in-one workspace often choose ClickUp over Notion, as ClickUp includes built-in time tracking while maintaining similar project and documentation capabilities.
