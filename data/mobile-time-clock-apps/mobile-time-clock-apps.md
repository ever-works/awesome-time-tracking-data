## Overview

Mobile time clock apps turn smartphones into portable time-tracking devices, essential for remote workers, field employees, and distributed teams who need to record hours from various locations.

## Key Features

### Core Functionality
- One-tap clock in/out
- Real-time sync with cloud systems
- Offline mode with later sync
- Break tracking
- Job/project selection
- Shift notes and comments

### Verification Technologies
- GPS location capture
- Geofencing (location-based restrictions)
- Photo capture at punch
- Facial recognition
- QR code scanning
- NFC tag reading

### Management Capabilities
- Live dashboard for supervisors
- Approve/reject time entries
- See who's currently working
- Real-time alerts for issues
- Export to payroll systems

## Common Use Cases

- Construction workers at job sites
- Healthcare workers doing home visits
- Field service technicians
- Delivery drivers
- Restaurant and retail employees
- Remote and hybrid workers

## Benefits

- Eliminates need for physical time clocks
- Reduces time theft and buddy punching
- Provides location proof
- Real-time attendance visibility
- Reduces administrative burden
- Lower hardware costs

## Challenges

- Requires employee smartphones (BYOD policies)
- Battery drain concerns
- Privacy considerations
- Cellular/WiFi connectivity needed
- Employee training required

## Popular Apps

ClockShark, Workyard, Timeero, ClockifyNow, TSheets (QuickBooks Time), Hubstaff, Deputy, and hundreds more serving various industries.

## Pricing Models

Typically $2-10 per user per month, with volume discounts. Some offer free tiers for small teams.
