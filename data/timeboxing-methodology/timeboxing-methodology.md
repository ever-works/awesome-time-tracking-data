## Overview

Timeboxing is a time management technique where you allocate fixed time periods (timeboxes) to planned activities with strict start and end times. It prevents work from expanding to fill available time and creates structure for the work day.

## How It Works

1. Plan your day by assigning specific time blocks to tasks
2. Set a fixed duration for each activity (the timebox)
3. Work on only that activity during the timebox
4. Stop when the time is up, regardless of completion
5. Review progress and adjust future timeboxes accordingly

## Key Principles

- Fixed time limits prevent perfectionism
- Scheduled blocks on calendar create commitment
- Parkinson's Law: work expands to fill available time
- Time constraints boost focus and efficiency
- Makes trade-offs and priorities explicit
- Prevents low-priority work from consuming time

## Benefits

- Improved time estimation skills
- Better work-life balance through protected time
- Reduced procrastination
- Visible daily structure
- Prevents overcommitment
- Forces prioritization decisions
- Creates realistic schedules

## Applications

Effective for project planning, meeting scheduling, deep work sessions, and balancing multiple priorities.
