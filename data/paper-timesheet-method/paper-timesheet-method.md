## Overview

Paper timesheets are the most traditional form of manual time tracking. Employees write down the time they start and finish each task on a paper timesheet with designated boxes for date, client name, work description, and hours/minutes.

## Advantages

- Simple and easy to use anywhere, anytime
- Inexpensive - only requires pen and paper
- No technology or internet required
- Familiar to all employees
- No learning curve

## Disadvantages

- Must physically distribute and collect timesheets
- Filling out paper timesheets is time-consuming
- Prone to errors, illegible handwriting, and lost sheets
- Difficult to aggregate and analyze data
- No automatic calculations
- Manual data entry required for payroll
- Easy to forget or procrastinate completion

## Modern Alternatives

While simple, most organizations have moved to digital time tracking for accuracy, efficiency, and data integration with payroll systems.

## Best For

Small teams, temporary projects, locations without reliable technology access, or as a backup system.

## Pricing

Minimal cost - only paper and writing implements required.
