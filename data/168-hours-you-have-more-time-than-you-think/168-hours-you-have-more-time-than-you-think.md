## Overview

168 Hours by Laura Vanderkam challenges the myth that we don't have enough time, showing that everyone has the same 168 hours per week and teaching how to use them more effectively.

## Core Philosophy

The book argues that the problem isn't lack of time, but lack of priorities. By examining how successful people use their 168 hours, Vanderkam identifies strategies anyone can use.

## Key Concepts

- **Time Tracking**: Understand where your time actually goes
- **Core Competencies**: Focus on what you do best
- **Outsourcing**: Delegate or eliminate tasks that aren't your strengths
- **Strategic Thinking**: Plan your week like a project manager
- **Work-Life Integration**: Replace balance with intentional integration
- **Small Moments**: Use small pockets of time effectively

## Methodology

Vanderkam recommends tracking your time for a week to see where hours really go, then redesigning your schedule based on priorities.

## Impact on Time Management

Shifts perspective from time scarcity to time abundance, empowering readers to make conscious choices about all 168 hours each week.
