## Overview

Contemporaneous time entry is the practice of recording time at or near the moment work is performed, rather than reconstructing time entries from memory hours, days, or weeks later.

## Why It's Required

**DCAA Requirements**: For government contractors, DCAA mandates that employees track time daily and in real time, logging time only for the current day.

**Legal Profession Standards**: Law firms must maintain contemporaneous time records to withstand scrutiny in fee disputes and to comply with ethical billing requirements.

**Accuracy**: Memory-based time reconstruction typically results in 15-25% under-reporting of billable time and inaccurate project allocation.

## Best Practices

- **Daily Entry**: Record time every day before leaving work
- **Real-Time Capture**: Log time immediately after completing tasks when possible
- **No Backdating**: Do not add entries for past days (except same-day corrections)
- **No Future Dating**: Only log time for work actually performed
- **Detailed Descriptions**: Include specific task descriptions while the work is fresh in memory

## Benefits

- Higher accuracy in time capture (studies show 20%+ improvement)
- Better client billing justification
- Improved project cost tracking
- Enhanced compliance with regulatory requirements
- More defensible time records in audits or disputes

## Enforcement

Many DCAA-compliant time tracking systems enforce contemporaneous entry by:
- Disabling entries for past dates
- Requiring supervisor approval for late corrections
- Flagging non-daily timekeepers in compliance reports
