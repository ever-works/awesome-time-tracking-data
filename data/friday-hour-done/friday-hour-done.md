## Overview

Friday Hour Done is a weekly review practice that complements Monday Hour One planning. It involves spending one hour at the end of each week to reflect, review, and create closure before the weekend.

## Purpose

The practice serves multiple functions:
- Celebrate wins and acknowledge accomplishments
- Review what worked and what didn't
- Process incomplete tasks
- Create psychological closure for the week
- Prepare mentally for the weekend
- Set the stage for next Monday's planning

## The Review Process

### Reflect on Accomplishments
- List everything completed during the week
- Acknowledge progress toward goals
- Celebrate wins, both large and small
- Recognize effort and growth

### Analyze What Worked
- Identify successful strategies and approaches
- Note what contributed to productivity
- Recognize helpful habits or systems
- Document insights for future application

### Review Challenges
- Examine what didn't go as planned
- Understand obstacles and setbacks
- Avoid self-judgment—focus on learning
- Identify areas for adjustment

### Process Incomplete Tasks
- Acknowledge unfinished items without guilt
- Decide what carries forward to next week
- Identify what can be delegated or eliminated
- Make peace with realistic capacity limits

## Key Benefits

- **Psychological closure**: Prevents work from mentally intruding on weekends
- **Continuous improvement**: Regular reflection drives incremental progress
- **Motivation**: Recognizing accomplishments builds momentum
- **Learning**: Analyzing patterns improves future planning
- **Stress reduction**: Processing incomplete tasks reduces anxiety
- **Better planning**: Insights inform next Monday Hour One session

## Integration with Monday Hour One

The weekly cycle:
- **Monday Hour One**: Plan the week ahead with intention
- **Daily execution**: Follow the plan with flexibility
- **Friday Hour Done**: Review, reflect, and close the week
- **Weekend rest**: Truly disconnect, knowing work is processed
- **Repeat**: Begin fresh Monday with Friday's insights

## Implementation Tips

### Schedule It
Block the last hour of Friday (or your last work day) as non-negotiable review time.

### Create Ritual
Use the same location, time, and process each week to build habit.

### Stay Balanced
Allocate time evenly:
- 20 minutes: Celebrate and acknowledge
- 20 minutes: Review and analyze
- 20 minutes: Process and prepare

### Document Insights
Keep a running log of learnings, patterns, and improvements to track growth over time.

### Be Honest
Accurate reflection requires honesty about successes and struggles.

## What to Review

### Productivity Metrics
- Tasks completed vs. planned
- Time estimates vs. actual
- Deep work hours achieved
- Meeting time consumed
- Distraction frequency

### Qualitative Factors
- Energy levels throughout week
- Quality of work produced
- Relationships and collaboration
- Stress and wellbeing
- Alignment with values and goals

### System Effectiveness
- Did Monday's plan serve you well?
- Which tools or methods helped?
- What adjustments would improve next week?
- Are you working on the right things?

## Common Mistakes

**Skipping It**: Consistency is key—don't sacrifice this for "urgent" work
**Being Too Critical**: Focus on learning, not self-judgment
**Rushing Through**: Give it the full hour for meaningful reflection
**Only Focusing on Failures**: Celebrate successes equally
**Not Taking Action**: Insights without adjustments waste the exercise

## Building the Habit

Start with 15 minutes and gradually expand to the full hour as the practice becomes natural.

## Long-term Impact

Regular Friday Hour Done practice develops:
- Self-awareness about work patterns
- Realistic planning capabilities
- Ability to let go and recover
- Continuous improvement mindset
- Better work-life boundaries
- Reduced burnout risk
