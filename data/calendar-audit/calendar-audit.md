## Overview

A Calendar Audit is a systematic review of how you spend time in meetings and scheduled activities. By analyzing your calendar over 2-4 weeks, you can identify patterns, eliminate unnecessary commitments, and reclaim time for focused work.

## How to Conduct a Calendar Audit

### Step 1: Collect Data (2-4 weeks)
- Review all calendar events from the past month
- Export calendar data if possible
- Include both work and personal calendars
- Note recurring vs. one-time events

### Step 2: Categorize Time
Label each meeting/event as:
- **Essential**: Required for your role, high value
- **Useful**: Helpful but not critical
- **Optional**: Could skip without major impact
- **Wasteful**: Low value, poor use of time

Also categorize by type:
- Strategy/planning meetings
- Status updates/check-ins
- Decision-making meetings
- Collaborative work sessions
- 1-on-1s
- All-hands/team meetings
- External meetings
- Focus time blocks

### Step 3: Analyze Patterns
- Calculate total hours in meetings per week
- Identify back-to-back meeting days
- Find recurring meetings that might be unnecessary
- Locate time fragmentation (too many short gaps)
- Spot patterns of meeting overload

### Step 4: Identify Improvements
**Meetings to Eliminate**:
- Recurring meetings that have outlived their purpose
- Meetings where your attendance isn't needed
- Status updates that could be async

**Meetings to Optimize**:
- Shorten 60-minute meetings to 45 minutes
- Combine multiple short check-ins
- Convert to async where possible
- Improve agendas and structure

**Time to Protect**:
- Block focus time for deep work
- Create no-meeting days or time blocks
- Add buffer time between meetings

### Step 5: Take Action
- Decline optional low-value meetings
- Propose shorter durations
- Suggest async alternatives
- Block focus time on calendar
- Communicate new meeting policies

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Total meeting hours per week**: Aim for <10 hours for individual contributors, <20 for managers
- **Meeting-free time blocks**: Should have daily 2+ hour blocks
- **Back-to-back meetings**: Minimize days with 4+ consecutive meetings
- **Meeting preparation time**: Ensure meetings have agendas and prep time
- **Focus time percentage**: Aim for 40-60% of week in focus time

## Red Flags to Watch For

- More than 50% of week in meetings
- No 2+ hour focus blocks in the week
- Recurring meetings without clear purpose
- Frequent last-minute meeting additions
- Meetings without agendas or outcomes
- Meetings with >8 people but no clear roles

## Benefits

- Reclaim 5-10 hours per week on average
- Reduce meeting fatigue
- Increase focus time for important work
- Improve meeting quality and effectiveness
- Better work-life boundaries
- Increased control over schedule

## Best Practices

- Conduct audits quarterly
- Track improvements over time
- Involve your team in meeting optimization
- Create team meeting guidelines
- Default to 25 or 50-minute meetings
- Require agendas for all meetings
- Make meeting attendance truly optional when possible
