## Overview

The Ultradian Rhythm Method leverages your body's natural 90-120 minute cycles of high and low alertness. By aligning work sessions with these biological rhythms rather than arbitrary time blocks, you can maintain peak focus while preventing fatigue and burnout.

## Scientific Background

Ultradian rhythms are recurrent periods or cycles repeated throughout a 24-hour day. Sleep researcher Nathan Kleitman discovered these 90-minute cycles during REM sleep, and later research found the same pattern governs our waking alertness.

## The 90-Minute Work Cycle

### Peak Performance Window
- First 90 minutes: Highest alertness and focus
- Natural cycle of energy and attention
- Body's optimal sustained concentration period
- Matches expert performers' practice sessions

### The Crash
- After 90 minutes: Natural energy dip
- Physical and mental fatigue signals
- Decreased focus and productivity
- Body's request for recovery

## Implementation

### Session Structure
- Work intensely for 90 minutes
- Take a 20-minute break
- Repeat cycle 3-4 times per day
- Total: 4.5-6 hours of deep work daily

### Break Activities
- Physical movement or walk
- Meditation or breathing exercises
- Light snack or hydration
- Social interaction
- Complete mental disengagement from work

## Differences from Pomodoro

- Pomodoro: 25-minute work, 5-minute breaks (arbitrary)
- Ultradian: 90-minute work, 20-minute breaks (biological)
- Pomodoro: More cycles, less deep work
- Ultradian: Fewer cycles, sustained focus

## Benefits

- Aligned with natural biology
- Sustained deep work periods
- Better for complex tasks
- Reduced mental fatigue
- Higher quality output
- Prevents burnout

## Best For

- Deep work requiring sustained focus
- Complex problem-solving
- Creative work
- Writing and research
- Strategic thinking
- Software development

## Challenges

- Requires schedule flexibility
- Difficult in interrupt-driven environments
- Takes time to identify personal cycle
- May not align with meeting schedules

## Finding Your Rhythm

Individual cycles vary 90-120 minutes. Track your energy levels for a week to identify your personal rhythm pattern.
