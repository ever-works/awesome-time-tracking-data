## Overview

PTO (Paid Time Off) accrual tracking manages how employees earn, use, and carry over paid leave. Proper tracking ensures compliance, prevents errors, and provides transparency to employees.

## Accrual Methods

### Per Pay Period
- Accrue fixed amount each pay period
- Example: 3.08 hours per biweekly pay period = 80 hours/year
- Most common method
- Easy to calculate and predict

### Anniversary-Based
- Full allocation on hire date anniversary
- "Use it or lose it" by next anniversary
- Simple but can lead to year-end rushes

### Hours-Worked Based
- Earn PTO based on hours actually worked
- Common for hourly/part-time employees
- Example: 1 hour PTO per 40 hours worked

### Unlimited PTO
- No accrual or balance tracking
- Manager approval required
- Growing trend in tech industry

## Key Tracking Elements

### What to Track
- **Accrual rate**: How much PTO earned per period
- **Current balance**: Hours available
- **Used hours**: Time off taken
- **Pending requests**: Requested but not approved
- **Scheduled time off**: Future approved leave
- **Carryover limits**: Maximum hours that roll to next year

### Policy Parameters
- Accrual caps (maximum balance)
- Carryover rules
- Waiting period for new hires
- Payout on termination
- Blackout dates
- Minimum/maximum request increments

## Compliance Considerations

### State Variations
- Some states require PTO payout at termination (e.g., California)
- "Use it or lose it" illegal in some states
- Accrual requirements vary
- Documentation standards differ

### Best Practices
- Clear written policies
- Accurate record-keeping
- Transparent employee access to balances
- Consistent policy application
- Regular audits

## Automation Benefits

- Automatic calculation of accruals
- Real-time balance updates
- Employee self-service portals
- Approval workflows
- Calendar integration
- Payroll system integration
- Compliance reporting

## Common Challenges

1. **Manual calculation errors**
2. **Inconsistent policy application**
3. **Employees unaware of balances**
4. **Forgotten carryover limits**
5. **Termination payout miscalculations**
