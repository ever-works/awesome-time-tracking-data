## Overview

Overtime calculation automation automatically identifies overtime hours and applies appropriate pay rates based on labor laws and company policies. The system tracks when employees exceed standard work hours and calculates overtime compensation according to federal, state, and organizational rules.

## Key Capabilities

### Threshold Detection
- Monitors hours against daily/weekly limits
- Identifies when overtime begins (e.g., after 40 hours/week)
- Tracks multiple overtime tiers (1.5x, 2x pay)
- Handles state-specific rules (e.g., California daily OT)

### Rate Application
- Applies correct overtime multipliers
- Calculates blended rates for multiple pay rates
- Handles shift differentials
- Manages premium pay scenarios

### Compliance Management
- Enforces FLSA overtime requirements
- Applies state-specific labor laws
- Tracks exempt vs. non-exempt employees
- Maintains documentation for audits

## Benefits

- Eliminates manual calculation errors
- Ensures labor law compliance
- Reduces payroll processing time
- Prevents costly compliance violations
- Provides accurate overtime forecasting

## Compliance Considerations

### Federal FLSA Rules
- 40-hour workweek threshold
- 1.5x pay rate for overtime
- Exempt employee classifications

### State Variations
- California: Daily overtime after 8 hours
- Colorado: Daily and weekly thresholds
- State-specific premium pay requirements

## Integration Points

- Payroll systems for wage calculation
- Time clocks for hour tracking
- Scheduling software for forecasting
- HR systems for employee classification
