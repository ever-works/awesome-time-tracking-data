## Overview

Eat That Frog is a time management method by Brian Tracy based on tackling your biggest, most important task (your 'frog') first thing in the morning before doing anything else. The concept comes from a Mark Twain quote: 'If it's your job to eat a frog, it's best to do it first thing in the morning.'

## Core Principle

Start each day by completing your most important, challenging, or impactful task - the one you're most likely to procrastinate on. If you eat a live frog first thing in the morning, nothing worse can happen to you the rest of the day.

## The 21 Methods

Brian Tracy's book outlines 21 ways to stop procrastinating:

1. **Set the Table**: Decide exactly what you want
2. **Plan Every Day in Advance**: Think on paper
3. **Apply the 80/20 Rule**: Focus on top 20% of activities
4. **Consider the Consequences**: Think long-term
5. **Practice Creative Procrastination**: Procrastinate on low-value tasks
6. **Use the ABCDE Method**: Prioritize by impact
7. **Focus on Key Result Areas**: Identify critical success factors
8. **Apply the Law of Three**: Find your three most important tasks
9. **Prepare Thoroughly**: Get everything ready before starting
10. **Upgrade Your Key Skills**: Continuous improvement
11. **Identify Your Key Constraints**: Find bottlenecks
12. **Take It One Oil Barrel at a Time**: Break big tasks into steps
13. **Put the Pressure on Yourself**: Create self-imposed deadlines
14. **Maximize Your Personal Powers**: Work during peak energy times
15. **Motivate Yourself into Action**: Be your own cheerleader
16. **Practice Creative Procrastination**: Delay low-value activities
17. **Do the Most Difficult Task First**: Eat that frog
18. **Slice and Dice the Task**: Break it into specific steps
19. **Create Large Chunks of Time**: Schedule uninterrupted blocks
20. **Develop a Sense of Urgency**: Move fast on key tasks
21. **Single Handle Every Task**: Work without interruption

## How to Identify Your Frog

Your frog is typically:
- The task with the biggest positive consequences if completed
- The task you're most tempted to procrastinate on
- The most challenging or complex item on your list
- The task that contributes most to your goals
- The activity that creates the most value

## Implementation Steps

1. **Evening Preparation**: Identify tomorrow's frog before bed
2. **Morning Execution**: Start work on frog first thing, no email/social media first
3. **Single-Task Focus**: Work on frog without interruption until complete
4. **Sense of Accomplishment**: Enjoy momentum from completing hardest task early
5. **Productive Day**: Use energy from frog completion for remaining tasks

## Benefits

- **Eliminates Procrastination**: Forces action on avoided tasks
- **Builds Momentum**: Early win energizes rest of day
- **Increases Productivity**: Hardest work done during peak energy
- **Reduces Stress**: No dreading the big task all day
- **Improves Focus**: Clear priority prevents scattered efforts
- **Accelerates Goal Achievement**: Consistent progress on what matters most

## Why It Works

- **Peak Energy**: Morning offers best mental clarity and willpower
- **Psychological Win**: Completing hard task builds confidence
- **Momentum**: Success breeds more success
- **Decision Fatigue Prevention**: Make one key decision (what's your frog)
- **Parkinson's Law**: Work expands to fill time, so tackle big items first

## Common Mistakes

- Checking email before eating frog
- Starting with easier tasks to 'warm up'
- Not clearly identifying the true frog
- Choosing multiple frogs (should be ONE)
- Giving up if interrupted (return to frog after interruption)

## Combining with Other Methods

- **With Eisenhower Matrix**: Frog is usually a Q1 or Q2 task
- **With Time Blocking**: Block first 2-3 hours for frog
- **With Pomodoro**: Use pomodoros to work on frog
- **With GTD**: Frog comes from your Next Actions or Projects list

## Digital Tools

- Most to-do apps allow starring/flagging your frog
- Calendar apps for blocking morning time
- Simple paper list with frog at top

## Best For

- Chronic procrastinators
- People who avoid difficult tasks
- Those wanting simple prioritization method
- Anyone struggling with overwhelm
- Morning people with high AM energy

## Not Ideal For

- Night owls (low morning energy)
- Jobs requiring immediate morning responsiveness
- Those working across time zones with early meetings

## Getting Started

1. Tonight: Identify tomorrow's frog (one task)
2. Tomorrow: Start on frog before checking email/messages
3. Work uninterrupted for 90-120 minutes
4. Complete or make significant progress
5. Enjoy productive momentum for rest of day
6. Repeat daily

## Related Concepts

- **One Thing**: Similar focus on single most important task
- **MIT (Most Important Task)**: Identifying and tackling key task
- **Deep Work First**: Scheduling deep work in morning
- **Peak Performance**: Working during biological prime time
