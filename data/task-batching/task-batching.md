## Overview

Task batching is a productivity strategy that involves grouping similar tasks together to complete in one time period, avoiding the mental cost of context switching.

## The Science

- Context switching can lead to **40% decrease in productivity** (American Psychological Association)
- Takes an average of **23 minutes 15 seconds** to regain focus after interruption (UC Irvine)
- Task batching promotes single-tasking by design

## Key Benefits

- **Enhanced Focus and Flow**: More likely to achieve flow state
- **Reduced Stress**: Minimizes interruptions and lowers stress
- **Measurable Productivity Gains**: Save average of 5 hours each week
- **Reduced Context Switching**: From 40 to 15 times per day
- **25% Productivity Increase**: Through focused work

## Task Batching vs. Multitasking

Multitasking requires constant switching between different work types, increasing mental fatigue and errors. Task batching focuses on one task category at a time, leading to better focus and efficiency.

## Implementation

- Categorize tasks by grouping similar activities (emails, phone calls, writing)
- Allocate dedicated time blocks for each task category
- Schedule based on peak productivity periods
- Reserve creative mornings for creative task batches
- Batch routine tasks during lower-energy periods
