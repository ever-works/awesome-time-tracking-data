## Overview

"Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World" by Cal Newport argues that the ability to focus without distraction on cognitively demanding tasks is becoming increasingly valuable and rare. The book provides strategies for cultivating deep work in a culture of constant distraction.

## Core Concepts

### Deep Work
Professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push cognitive capabilities to their limit. These efforts create new value, improve skills, and are hard to replicate.

### Shallow Work
Non-cognitively demanding, logistical-style tasks often performed while distracted. These efforts tend not to create much new value and are easy to replicate.

## The Four Rules

### Rule #1: Work Deeply
Develop routines and rituals that support consistent deep work:
- **Monastic** - Eliminate or radically minimize shallow obligations
- **Bimodal** - Dedicate clearly defined stretches to deep pursuits
- **Rhythmic** - Make deep work a regular habit (same time daily)
- **Journalistic** - Fit deep work wherever you can into schedule

### Rule #2: Embrace Boredom
Train your ability to concentrate:
- Schedule breaks from focus (not breaks from distraction)
- Work like Teddy Roosevelt with intense bursts
- Practice productive meditation during physical activities
- Don't take breaks from distraction; take breaks from focus

### Rule #3: Quit Social Media
Apply deliberate practice to network tool selection:
- Use the 80/20 rule - only use tools that substantially benefit key activities
- Take 30-day breaks from tools to assess their value
- Don't use the internet to entertain yourself

### Rule #4: Drain the Shallows
Reduce shallow work:
- Schedule every minute of your day
- Quantify depth of activities
- Ask boss for shallow work budget
- Finish work by 5:30pm (fixed-schedule productivity)
- Become hard to reach

## Key Strategies

- **Shutdown Ritual** - Complete shutdown of work thinking each evening
- **Time Blocking** - Plan every minute of your day in advance
- **Deep Work Scoreboard** - Track hours of deep work achieved
- **4DX Framework** - Focus on wildly important, act on lead measures, keep compelling scoreboard, create cadence of accountability
