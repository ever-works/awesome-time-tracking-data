## Overview

Big Stretch Reminder is an intuitive Windows reminder tool designed to prompt users to take well-timed breaks. It's a lightweight break reminder for people who spend long hours at a computer, helping to prevent Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI) and encourage healthier work habits.

## Key Features

### Intelligent Timing
- Takes your activity and idle time into account
- Reminders happen when they're more likely to be helpful and less likely to be annoying
- Default setting is 50 minutes for break reminders (customizable)
- Pauses when you're already away from the computer

### Notification Options
- **Notifications**: Subtle taskbar notifications
- **Popups**: More prominent popup reminders
- **Timed Breaks**: Full-screen break enforcement
- **Taskbar Flash**: Subtle taskbar flash for minimal disruption

### Customization
- Display random tips to avoid RSI (default)
- Show motivational quotes instead
- Create custom messages
- Adjust break frequency and duration
- Choose notification style

### Audio Alerts
- Play audio alerts when breaks are due
- 4 built-in sounds included
- Support for custom sounds in multiple formats:
  - MP3, MP2, MP4
  - OGG, WAV, FLAC
  - OGA, AC3

## History

First released in 2006 by Matt Lester, Big Stretch Reminder has been helping Windows users maintain healthy computer habits for nearly two decades.

- Office workers

## Philosophy

Big Stretch Reminder follows the principle that regular small breaks are more effective than infrequent long breaks. By prompting users at appropriate times based on their activity, it helps establish healthier computer usage patterns without being intrusive.
