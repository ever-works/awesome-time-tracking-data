## Definitions

### Deep Work
Cognitively demanding activities requiring focused attention:
- Strategic thinking
- Complex problem-solving
- Writing/creating
- Learning new skills
- Analysis and research
- Coding/designing

### Shallow Work
Logistical, administrative tasks requiring minimal focus:
- Email processing
- Scheduling
- Status updates  
- Routine meetings
- Data entry
- Filing/organizing

## Ideal Ratio

**Knowledge Workers**:
- Target: 60-80% deep work
- Reality: Often 30-50% deep work
- Gap represents major productivity opportunity

**By Role**:
- Individual contributors: 70-80% deep
- Mid-managers: 50-60% deep
- Executives: 30-40% deep (more coordination)

## Measuring Your Ratio

**Method 1: Time Tracking**
1. Track all time for 1-2 weeks
2. Categorize as deep or shallow
3. Calculate percentage
4. Identify improvement areas

**Method 2: Calendar Analysis**
- Review past month calendar
- Classify each block
- Count hours of each type
- Calculate ratio

## Improving the Ratio

### Increase Deep Work
- Time block focus sessions
- Protect morning hours
- Create meeting-free days
- Batch deep work
- Eliminate distractions

### Reduce Shallow Work
**Eliminate**:
- Unnecessary meetings
- Low-value tasks
- Redundant processes

**Automate**:
- Email filters/templates
- Scheduling tools (Calendly)
- Status reporting

**Delegate**:
- Administrative tasks
- Low-skill work
- Non-core activities

**Batch**:
- Email processing (2-3x daily)
- Admin time (Friday PM)
- Meetings (specific days)

## 2026 Research

Study of 500,000+ hours:
- Only 51% time in deep work tools
- 34% in communication
- 15% in meetings
- Shows opportunity for improvement

## Warning Signs

- Deep work <40% of time
- No blocks longer than 2 hours
- Constant meeting interruptions
- Feeling busy but unproductive
- Important work always pushed

## Cal Newport's Approach

- Schedule every minute of day
- Maximize deep work blocks
- Strictly limit shallow work
- Fixed-schedule productivity
- Clear boundaries
