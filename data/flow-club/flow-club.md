## Overview

Flow Club offers virtual coworking sessions designed to drop you into productive flow. The platform is described as "Peloton for coworking" by cofounder and CEO Ricky Yean.

## How It Works

Users join a video session for a certain amount of time, and use the majority of that time to work on specific projects – together, but virtually. They may kick off the session to explain what they're going to work on, then at the end, they will recap the tasks they have accomplished.

## Key Features and Methods

Flow Club combines popular methods that are shown to help with deep work focus and boost productivity, with the primary concept being body doubling. The method is based on the popular Pomodoro Technique, with the most popular session length being 50 minutes.

The software enables anyone to create a successful co-working experience, from scheduling logistics to creating a productive video environment complete with:

- Built-in timers
- Background music options
- Integrated to-do lists
- Ways to celebrate progress

## Community and Benefits

Over half of Flow Club users are neurodivergent, and the platform offers special features to aid focus, such as:

- Optional background music
- Choice of verbal or non-verbal sessions
- Supportive community environment

Users attend an average of 10 to 11 sessions weekly.

## Growth and Pricing

Flow Club quadrupled users since the beginning of 2023 and now offers close to 2,000 sessions a week for nearly round-the-clock coverage.

The service costs $400 a year after a free two-week trial.

## Target Audience

Particularly beneficial for neurodivergent individuals, remote workers, and anyone seeking structured accountability and focus support.
