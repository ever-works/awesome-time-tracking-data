## Overview

The Eat the Frog method was made well-known by productivity consultant Brian Tracy in his book "Eat That Frog!: 21 Great Ways to Stop Procrastinating and Get More Done in Less Time." The technique is based on a Mark Twain quote: if the first thing you do each morning is eat a live frog, you can go through the day with the satisfaction of knowing that it's probably the worst thing that will happen to you all day.

## Core Concept

Your "frog" is your biggest, most important task—the one you are most likely to procrastinate on if you don't do something about it. The key is developing the lifelong habit of tackling your major task first thing each morning, before you do anything else and without taking too much time to think about it.

## Key Principles

### The First Rule
If you have to eat two frogs, eat the ugliest one first. This means if you have two important tasks, start with the biggest, hardest, and most important one.

### The ABCDE Method
Tracy's system for prioritizing tasks:
- **A tasks**: Things you must do (your frogs)
- **B tasks**: Things you should do
- **C tasks**: Nice-to-dos
- **D tasks**: Tasks you should delegate
- **E tasks**: Tasks you should eliminate

## Benefits

- Combats procrastination by encouraging immediate action on important tasks
- Creates sense of accomplishment early in the day
- Frees mental energy from worrying about difficult tasks
- Increases productivity on highest-impact work
- Prevents important tasks from being displaced by urgent but less important work

## Why It Works

In today's knowledge economy, the most valuable work requires all mental resources focused on one thing—cognitively demanding tasks like coding, designing, writing, strategizing, and problem-solving. By tackling these first, you use your peak mental energy on what matters most.
