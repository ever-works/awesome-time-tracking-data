## Overview

The 90-minute ultradian cycle represents the natural rhythm of human alertness and performance. Within each 90-120 minute period, the brain reaches a performance peak followed by a natural recovery trough, making this the optimal unit for structuring focused work sessions.

## Scientific Basis

Research on ultradian rhythms shows:
- Natural cycles repeat every 90-120 minutes
- Performance peaks last approximately 90 minutes
- Recovery troughs require 15-20 minutes
- Elite performers naturally align with these rhythms
- Top violinists practice maximum 4.5 hours daily in 90-minute bursts

## Implementation

### Basic Structure
- Work in focused 90-minute blocks
- Take 15-20 minute breaks between blocks
- Limit to 2-3 blocks per day for sustainable performance
- Honor recovery periods as essential, not optional

## Benefits

- 50% reduction in mental fatigue
- Higher accuracy on complex tasks
- Sustainable long-term productivity
- Prevention of burnout
- Natural work-rest balance
