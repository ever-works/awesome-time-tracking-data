## Overview

The Weekly Review is a cornerstone practice from David Allen's Getting Things Done (GTD) methodology. It's a dedicated time (typically 1-2 hours) to review the past week, process all inputs, update your systems, and prepare for the week ahead.

## Purpose

The Weekly Review ensures:
- All commitments are captured and current
- Your system is trustworthy and up-to-date
- You have clarity on priorities and next actions
- Nothing falls through the cracks
- You maintain perspective on projects and goals

## The Classic GTD Weekly Review Steps

**1. Get Clear (Empty Your Head)**
- Collect loose papers and materials
- Process notes from meetings and events
- Empty physical and digital inboxes
- Capture any open loops or concerns

**2. Get Current (Update Your Lists)**
- Review action lists and check off completed items
- Review project list and add any new projects
- Review waiting-for list and follow up as needed
- Review calendar for past and upcoming weeks
- Update context lists (@home, @office, @calls, etc.)

**3. Get Creative (Look Ahead)**
- Review someday/maybe list for activation
- Review goals and larger objectives
- Identify projects from your roles and responsibilities
- Be creative about new opportunities
- Capture any new ideas or insights

## Timing and Frequency

**When to Conduct**
- Friday afternoon (popular choice)
- Sunday evening (week preparation)
- Saturday morning (weekend start)
- Choose consistent day and time

**Duration**
- 1-2 hours typical
- Longer when catching up
- Shorter with practice and maintenance

**Frequency**
- Weekly minimum
- Some add monthly review
- Quarterly reviews for bigger picture

## Extended Weekly Review Components

**Review Past Week**
- What did I accomplish?
- What went well?
- What could improve?
- What did I learn?
- Wins to celebrate?

**Review Calendar**
- Past week: capture missed actions
- Next 2-4 weeks: prepare for commitments
- Flag important dates
- Identify preparation needs

**Review Projects**
- Active projects: identify next actions
- Stalled projects: determine blocks
- Completed projects: celebrate and archive
- New projects: capture and define

**Review Areas of Responsibility**
- Work roles
- Personal responsibilities
- Relationships
- Health and wellness
- Finance
- Personal development

**Process Inputs**
- Email inboxes
- Physical inboxes
- Notes and ideas
- Messages and communications
- Receipts and documents

**Clean and Organize**
- Clear desk and workspace
- File loose items
- Update reference materials
- Organize digital files
- Clear downloads folder

**Plan Ahead**
- Identify priorities for coming week
- Schedule important tasks
- Prepare materials needed
- Block focus time
- Identify potential obstacles

## Benefits

- **Clarity**: Know exactly what's on your plate
- **Trust**: Confidence in your system
- **Perspective**: See the bigger picture
- **Reduced Stress**: Nothing lurking in the shadows
- **Proactive Planning**: Get ahead of commitments
- **Better Decisions**: Full context for choices
- **Completion**: Sense of progress and accomplishment

## Common Challenges

**Time Scarcity**
- Solution: Schedule as non-negotiable appointment
- Start with 30-minute version if needed

**Inconsistency**
- Solution: Same time and place each week
- Set calendar reminders
- Track streak to build habit

**Overwhelming**
- Solution: Use checklist to guide process
- Focus on essentials first
- Build up comprehensiveness over time

**Interruptions**
- Solution: Find quiet time and place
- Turn off notifications
- Communicate boundaries

- [ ] Process notes from meetings
- [ ] Review and update project list
- [ ] Review and update action lists
- [ ] Review waiting-for list
- [ ] Review someday/maybe list
- [ ] Review last week's calendar
- [ ] Review next 2-4 weeks calendar
- [ ] Update goals and objectives
- [ ] Clean and organize workspace
- [ ] Plan priorities for next week

## Making It Sustainable

**Create Ritual**
- Same time and place
- Prepare environment (coffee, music)
- Use checklist for consistency

**Keep It Enjoyable**
- Pleasant workspace
- Favorite beverage
- Celebrate wins
- Reflection time as gift to yourself

**Adjust to Fit**
- Customize for your role and needs
- Add/remove steps as appropriate
- Find optimal duration
- Experiment with timing

**Track Completion**
- Mark completed reviews on calendar
- Build streak motivation
- Note benefits experienced

## Integration with Other Systems

- Works with any task management tool
- Complements time blocking
- Supports Agile sprint planning
- Enhances goal-setting systems
- Fundamental to GTD methodology
