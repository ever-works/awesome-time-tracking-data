## Overview

Starting December 31, 2025, New York requires all contractors to electronically submit certified payroll records through a dedicated state portal. This requirement applies to contracts awarded after June 16, 2026, and represents the biggest administrative change for construction contractors working on government-funded projects.

## Key Requirements

### Electronic Submission Mandate

- All certified payroll records must be filed electronically
- Submissions made through dedicated New York State portal
- Paper submissions no longer accepted for new contracts
- Real-time compliance tracking by state authorities

### Affected Projects

- Public works construction contracts
- Government-funded building projects
- Prevailing wage-covered work in New York State
- Both state and local government projects

## Implementation Timeline

- **December 31, 2025**: Electronic filing requirement effective
- **June 16, 2026**: Applies to all contracts awarded after this date
- **2026**: Full transition period for contractors to adapt systems

## Technology Requirements

### Contractors Must:

- Adopt electronic time tracking systems compatible with state portal
- Ensure accurate classification tracking by job role
- Maintain digital records of all hours worked
- Implement systems for real-time payroll data capture

### Recommended Solutions

- Construction-specific time tracking software with certified payroll features
- GPS time tracking for jobsite verification
- Integration capabilities with state submission portal
- Automated WH-347 form generation

## Compliance Challenges

### Administrative Burden

- Learning new electronic filing systems
- Training staff on new procedures
- Ensuring data accuracy before submission
- Managing multiple project submissions

### Technology Adoption

- Upgrading from paper-based systems
- Integrating with existing payroll software
- Ensuring mobile accessibility for field teams
- Data security and privacy compliance

## Best Practices for 2026

1. **Adopt Specialized Software**: Use construction time tracking tools with built-in certified payroll compliance
2. **Train Your Team**: Ensure all staff understand new electronic requirements
3. **Test Systems Early**: Pilot electronic submission before it's required
4. **Maintain Backup Records**: Keep detailed digital and physical records
5. **Conduct Regular Audits**: Verify data accuracy before submission

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

- Withholding of contract payments
- Fines and penalties from New York State Department of Labor
- Potential debarment from future government contracts
- Back wages owed to workers if discrepancies found

## National Trend

New York's electronic filing requirement is part of a broader national trend toward digital compliance in construction. Other states are expected to follow with similar mandates in coming years.
