## Overview

Time Confetti refers to the fragmentation of the workday into small, scattered pieces that are too short to accomplish meaningful work. This modern workplace challenge results from excessive meetings, constant interruptions, and poor calendar management.

## The Problem

A typical knowledge worker's calendar:
- Meeting 9:00-9:30
- 30-minute gap
- Meeting 10:00-11:00
- 45-minute gap  
- Lunch meeting 12:00-1:00
- 30-minute gap
- Meeting 1:30-2:00
- etc.

These gaps are too short for:
- Deep work requiring sustained concentration
- Complex problem-solving
- Creative thinking
- Writing substantial content
- Meaningful project progress

## Why It Matters

Research shows:
- **25 minutes** to regain focus after an interruption
- **90 minutes** minimum for productive deep work
- **Fragmented time** is only suitable for shallow tasks
- **Meeting recovery time** often needed after calls
- **Context switching** taxes cognitive resources

## Causes

1. **Meeting Culture**: Default to 30 or 60-minute slots
2. **Calendar Tetris**: Fitting meetings into any available gap
3. **Reactive Scheduling**: Accepting any meeting invite
4. **Lack of Boundaries**: No protected focus time
5. **Synchronous Bias**: Preference for meetings over async communication
6. **Status Signaling**: Full calendar seen as importance indicator

## Solutions

### Individual Level
- **Time Blocking**: Block 2-4 hour chunks for focus work
- **Meeting Batching**: Cluster meetings on specific days
- **Default No**: Decline meetings without clear purpose
- **Office Hours**: Set specific times for ad-hoc meetings
- **Meeting-Free Days**: Designate entire days for deep work
- **Buffer Time**: Schedule 15-min breaks between meetings

### Team Level
- **Meeting Audits**: Review necessity of recurring meetings
- **Async First**: Default to written communication
- **Core Collaboration Hours**: Limit meetings to specific windows
- **Shorter Defaults**: 25 and 50-minute meetings, not 30 and 60
- **Focus Time Norms**: Company-wide no-meeting blocks

### Calendar Strategies
- **Minimum Block Size**: Don't leave gaps under 90 minutes
- **Consolidate Meetings**: Stack back-to-back rather than spread
- **Decline Early**: Say no before calendar fills up
- **Preemptive Blocking**: Block focus time before meetings arrive
- **Visual Signals**: Use calendar colors to highlight problem patterns

## Measurement

Track your Time Confetti metrics:
- Average uninterrupted block size
- Number of focus blocks (90+ min) per week
- Percentage of day in meetings
- Number of context switches per day
- Time between meetings (recovery gaps)

## The Goal

Aim for:
- At least **2-3 blocks of 2+ hours** per week for deep work
- **Maximum 50%** of time in meetings
- **Minimum 90-minute gaps** when possible
- **Meeting-free mornings** or full days
- **Consolidated meeting days** (all meetings on specific days)
