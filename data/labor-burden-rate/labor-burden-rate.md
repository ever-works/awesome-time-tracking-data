## Overview

Labor Burden Rate represents the hidden costs of employment beyond base salary, critical for understanding true labor expenses and setting profitable billing rates.

## Components

### Mandatory Costs
- FICA/Payroll taxes (7.65%)
- Unemployment insurance (varies)
- Workers' compensation (varies)
- Required benefits

### Voluntary Benefits
- Health insurance
- Retirement contributions
- Life/disability insurance
- PTO accrual cost

### Indirect Costs
- Office space allocation
- Equipment/technology
- Software licenses
- Utilities
- Administrative support

## Calculation Example

**Base Salary**: $60,000

**Additional Costs**:
- Payroll taxes: $4,590 (7.65%)
- Benefits: $12,000 (20%)
- Overhead: $8,000 (13.3%)

**Total Cost**: $84,590
**Burden Rate**: 1.41x
**Burden Percentage**: 41%

## Industry Benchmarks

**Low Burden** (1.25-1.35x):
- Minimal benefits
- Low overhead
- Home-based business

**Average Burden** (1.35-1.60x):
- Standard benefits package
- Normal office overhead
- Typical professional services

**High Burden** (1.60-2.0x+):
- Comprehensive benefits
- High overhead (prime office space)
- Government contractors
- Regulated industries

## Application in Time Tracking

**Cost Calculation**:
- Employee base rate: $30/hour
- Burden multiplier: 1.5x
- True cost: $45/hour
- 10 hours tracked = $450 actual cost

**Pricing Decisions**:
- True cost: $45/hour
- Desired margin: 40%

## Regional Variations

Burden rates vary by location due to:
- State/local taxes
- Workers' comp rates
- Benefits costs
- Real estate expenses
- Cost of living
