## Overview

The Ivy Lee Method is a century-old productivity system that has stood the test of time due to its simplicity and effectiveness. It helps people focus on what truly matters by limiting daily tasks and enforcing priority-based execution.

## Origin

In 1918, Charles M. Schwab, president of Bethlehem Steel Corporation, brought in productivity consultant Ivy Lee to help increase his team's efficiency. Lee's method was so effective that Schwab later sent him a check for $25,000 (equivalent to about $400,000 today).

## The Method

1. **At the end of each workday**, write down the six most important tasks for the following day
2. **Prioritize those six items** in order of their true importance
3. **The next morning**, concentrate only on the first task
4. **Work on it until it's finished** before moving to the second task
5. **Approach the rest of the list** in the same fashion
6. **At the end of the day**, move any unfinished items to a new list of six tasks for the following day
7. **Repeat the process** every working day

## Key Principles

- **Limit to six tasks**: Forces you to prioritize ruthlessly and focus on what truly matters
- **One task at a time**: Prevents context-switching and maintains deep focus
- **Priority-based execution**: Most important work gets done first when energy is highest
- **Simple and sustainable**: No complex systems or tools required

## Why It Works

- **Reduces decision fatigue**: Planning is done the night before, not in the moment
- **Creates constraints**: The six-task limit prevents overcommitment
- **Eliminates multitasking**: Forces single-tasking for better quality and efficiency
- **Builds consistency**: Daily practice creates productive habits
- **Provides closure**: Completing tasks in order provides satisfaction and momentum

## Modern Application

While originally designed for industrial executives, the Ivy Lee Method works equally well for knowledge workers, entrepreneurs, and anyone seeking a simple, effective productivity system.
