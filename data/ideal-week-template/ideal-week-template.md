## Overview

The Ideal Week method involves creating a template for your perfect week, blocking time for all important activities (deep work, meetings, exercise, family), then using it as a north star to guide actual scheduling decisions.

## How to Create Your Ideal Week

### Step 1: List All Responsibilities
- Work projects and deep work
- Meetings (recurring)
- Email and communication
- Exercise and health
- Family and social time
- Personal development
- Rest and recovery

### Step 2: Estimate Time Needed
- How many hours/week for each?
- When is optimal time for each?
- What's non-negotiable vs. flexible?

### Step 3: Design the Template
- Block out non-negotiables first
- Add high-priority items during peak energy
- Schedule similar activities together
- Include buffer time
- Leave some unscheduled time

### Step 4: Test and Iterate
- Try for 2-4 weeks
- Note what works and doesn't
- Adjust template
- Refine over time

## Example Ideal Week

**Monday**: Planning, strategic work
**Tuesday**: Deep creative work
**Wednesday**: Meetings day
**Thursday**: Deep work, client calls
**Friday**: Wrap-up, admin, planning

## Benefits

- Intentional time allocation
- Balanced life by design
- Reference point for decisions
- Protects important activities
- Reduces decision fatigue

## Common Mistakes

- Over-scheduling (leave buffer)
- Ignoring energy patterns
- Too rigid (allow flexibility)
- Not accounting for real constraints
