## Overview

Execution by Larry Bossidy and Ram Charan addresses the gap between strategy and results. The authors argue that execution is not just the tactical component of business — it is a systematic process of making strategic decisions and following through on them.

## Core Arguments

- Many organizations overemphasize strategy while neglecting execution
- Executive leaders must be deeply involved in the execution process, not just setting direction
- Execution is a leader's most important job

## Three Core Processes

- **People Process**: Ensuring the right people are in the right roles and evaluated honestly
- **Strategy Process**: Translating vision into actionable plans with clear milestones
- **Operations Process**: Implementing the strategy through disciplined follow-through and accountability

## Culture of Execution

The book emphasizes that building a culture of execution requires:
- Leaders who know their people and business
- Insistence on realism in all discussions
- Setting clear goals and following up relentlessly
- Rewarding execution, not just planning
