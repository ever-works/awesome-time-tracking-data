## Overview

Calendar blocking (also called time blocking) involves scheduling specific blocks of time on your calendar for focused work on particular tasks or types of activities.

## Core Principles

- Treat tasks like appointments
- Reserve time in advance
- Protect blocked time from interruptions
- Include buffer time between blocks
- Review and adjust weekly

## Types of Blocks

### Focus Blocks
- Deep work on important projects
- 2-4 hour uninterrupted sessions
- Morning typically best

### Theme Blocks
- Similar tasks grouped together
- Context switching minimized
- Batch processing

### Buffer Blocks
- Catch-up time
- Unexpected issues
- 20% of schedule

## Benefits

- Transforms intentions into commitments
- Visual overview of time allocation
- Reduces decision fatigue
- Prevents over-commitment
- Increases accountability

## Best Practices

1. Block most important tasks first
2. Include realistic buffers
3. Color-code for clarity
4. Review weekly and adjust
5. Protect blocks from meetings
