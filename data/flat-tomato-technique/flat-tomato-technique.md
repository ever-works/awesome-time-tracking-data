## Overview

Flat Tomato is a variation of the Pomodoro Technique that emphasizes completing one task completely (making it "flat" or done) before moving to the next, rather than working on multiple tasks across Pomodoros.

## How It Works

1. Choose ONE task to complete
2. Work in focused sessions (typically 25-50 min)
3. Take short breaks between sessions
4. Continue until task is fully complete (flat)
5. Only then move to next task

## Key Principles

- **Single-tasking**: One task at a time
- **Completion focus**: Finish before switching
- **No multi-tasking**: Even across Pomodoros
- **Full attention**: Deep focus on current task

## Benefits

- Reduces context switching
- Increases task completion rate
- Deeper focus and flow states
- Sense of accomplishment
- Clearer progress tracking

## vs. Traditional Pomodoro

- Traditional: Work on Task A for 4 Pomodoros, then Task B
- Flat Tomato: Complete Task A entirely, then start Task B
