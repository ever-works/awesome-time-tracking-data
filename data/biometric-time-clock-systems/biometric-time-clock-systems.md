## Overview

Biometric time clock systems use unique physical characteristics to authenticate employees during clock-in/clock-out, providing the most accurate and secure method of time and attendance tracking available.

## Biometric Technologies

### Fingerprint Recognition
- Most common biometric method
- Scans unique fingerprint patterns
- Fast authentication (under 1 second)
- High accuracy rates (99.9%+)
- Handles wet or dry fingers

### Facial Recognition
- Contactless authentication
- Camera-based scanning
- Works in various lighting conditions
- Mask detection capabilities
- 3D liveness detection

### Iris Recognition
- Highest accuracy biometric method
- Scans unique iris patterns
- Works from a distance
- Not affected by glasses
- Extremely difficult to spoof

### Hand Geometry
- Measures hand shape and size
- Large scanning surface
- Works with gloves (some models)
- Suitable for industrial environments

## Key Benefits

- Eliminates buddy punching (saves 2-8% on payroll)
- No lost or shared badges/passwords
- Audit trail for compliance
- Real-time attendance data
- Reduces administrative time
- Integration with payroll systems

## Features

- Multi-factor authentication options
- Offline operation capability
- Cloud and on-premise deployment
- Mobile management apps
- Automated overtime alerts
- Department/job code tracking
- Shift scheduling
- PTO/absence management

## Privacy & Compliance

- Biometric data stored as mathematical templates
- Cannot be reverse-engineered
- GDPR, CCPA, BIPA compliant options
- Employee consent management
- Data encryption and secure storage

## Implementation Considerations

- Initial enrollment time required
- Privacy regulations vary by location
- Hardware costs higher than badge systems
- Network infrastructure requirements
- Integration with existing systems
