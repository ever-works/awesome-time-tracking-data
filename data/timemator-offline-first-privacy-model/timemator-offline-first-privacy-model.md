## Privacy-First Architecture

Timemator's fundamental design principle: "What happens on your computer stays on your computer." All your private data remains on your device and is never sent to Timemator's servers.

## Local Data Storage

### What Stays Local
- All tracked time sessions
- Project and task information
- Revenue and billing data
- Auto-tracking rules
- Application usage history
- Custom configurations

### No Cloud Upload
Timemator does NOT:
- Send data to external servers
- Store your information in the cloud
- Access your data remotely
- Share data with third parties
- Require account creation

## Sync Between Your Devices

### iCloud Personal Sync
Timemator uses Apple's iCloud to sync:
- Tasks and folders
- Time sessions
- Auto-tracking rules
- Settings and preferences

### Key Point
- Data syncs between YOUR Macs only
- Uses YOUR iCloud account
- Never touches Timemator's servers
- You control the data
- Can disable sync entirely

## Automatic Time Tracking

### Rule-Based Automation
Timemator automates tracking by:
1. Monitor files, applications, websites you use
2. Match against your predefined rules
3. Automatically start timer for matching work
4. All processing happens locally

### Privacy Advantage
- Application monitoring stays on device
- Website tracking never leaves your Mac
- File access patterns private
- No external visibility

## Comparison to Cloud-Based Tools

### Cloud-Based Time Tracking
**Examples**: Toggl, Harvest, Clockify

**Data Model**:
- All data stored in vendor's cloud
- Accessible from anywhere
- Company has access to your data
- Subject to vendor security
- Risk of data breaches

**Advantages**:
- Access from any device
- Team collaboration
- Automatic backups
- Mobile apps

**Disadvantages**:
- Privacy concerns
- Vendor dependency
- Potential data mining
- Subscription lock-in

### Timemator's Local Model
**Data Model**:
- Data on your devices only
- You control backups
- No vendor access
- Your iCloud security

**Advantages**:
- Complete privacy
- Data ownership
- No ongoing server dependency
- Works offline

**Disadvantages**:
- Mac-only (no Windows/web)
- Limited cross-device access
- Manual backup responsibility
- Solo user focus
