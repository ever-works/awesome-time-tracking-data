## Overview

Inbox Zero, developed by productivity expert Merlin Mann, is not about having zero emails, but about spending zero time thinking about your inbox. It's a rigorous approach to email management that emphasizes rapid decision-making and clear processing.

## The Five Actions

For each email, quickly decide ONE action:

### 1. Delete (or Archive)
Most emails don't require action. Remove immediately.

### 2. Delegate
Forward to appropriate person with clear instruction.

### 3. Respond
If takes < 2 minutes, respond immediately and archive.

### 4. Defer
Move to task system for later action, archive email.

### 5. Do
For longer responses, schedule dedicated time and process.

## Core Principles

### Process to Zero
Process entire inbox in session, don't leave messages "to deal with later."

### Touch Once
Make decision on first read, don't re-read multiple times.

### Use Folders Sparingly
Minimal folder structure: Archive and maybe 1-2 action folders.

### Batch Processing
Check email 2-3 times daily, process completely each time.

## Implementation

### Initial Clean-Out
1. Declare email bankruptcy (archive or delete everything older than 30 days)
2. Process remaining messages using five actions
3. Get to zero

### Daily Maintenance
1. Schedule email processing times (e.g., 9am, 1pm, 4pm)
2. Process to zero each time
3. Turn off email between processing times

## Benefits

- Reduced email anxiety
- Faster decision-making
- Clearer task management
- Less context switching
- Improved response times

## Common Misconceptions

### "I must respond immediately"
Inbox Zero allows scheduled processing times, not continuous monitoring.

### "Zero emails means I'm done"
Zero means processed and decided, not that all actions are complete.

### "It takes too long"
Initial setup takes time, but daily maintenance is faster than constant checking.
