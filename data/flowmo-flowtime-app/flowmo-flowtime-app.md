## Overview

FlowMo is a productivity application that implements the Flowtime Technique, offering a more flexible alternative to the rigid Pomodoro method. Instead of fixed 25-minute intervals, FlowMo allows users to work for as long as they maintain genuine focus, automatically calculating appropriate break lengths based on work duration.

## The Flowtime Technique

### Core Concept
Work until your focus naturally fades, then take a break proportional to your work session. This respects individual flow states and task variability while maintaining the break discipline that prevents burnout.

### Break Calculation
FlowMo uses the 20% rule:
- **30 minutes work** → 6-minute break
- **60 minutes work** → 12-minute break
- **90 minutes work** → 18-minute break
- **120 minutes work** → 24-minute break

### Advantages Over Pomodoro

**Respects Flow State**:
- Doesn't interrupt when deeply engaged
- Allows natural task completion
- Preserves momentum on complex work
- Reduces artificial breaks

**Task-Appropriate**:
- Some tasks need 15 minutes, others need 90
- Match session length to work type
- No forcing tasks into 25-minute boxes
- More realistic for variable work

**Reduces Frustration**:
- No timer anxiety
- No breaking during breakthroughs
- Natural stopping points
- Less rigid structure stress

## Flowtime vs. Pomodoro Comparison

### When Flowtime Works Better

**Creative Work**:
- Writing, design, coding
- Tasks requiring flow state
- Complex problem-solving
- Original idea generation

**Variable-Length Tasks**:
- Research and exploration
- Reading and analysis
- Strategic thinking
- Learning new concepts

**Deep Focus Work**:
- When interruptions are costly
- Building complex mental models
- Maintaining context

### When Pomodoro Works Better

**Procrastination-Prone Work**:
- Tasks you avoid
- Boring but necessary work
- When you need artificial deadlines

**Highly Distractible Situations**:
- Noisy environments
- Many interruptions
- Difficulty starting work

**Structured Preferences**:
- People who thrive on routine
- Those who need firm boundaries
- When external accountability helps

## Hybrid Approach: Flowmodoro

FlowMo also supports "Flowmodoro"—combining both methods:
- Use Flowtime for deep work sessions
- Calculate break as % of work time (Flowtime rule)
- But consider using shorter work sessions when focus is difficult
- Flexibility in session length, discipline in breaks

## Implementation Best Practices

### Starting Out
1. **Experiment**: Try different session lengths
2. **Track Patterns**: Note when focus naturally ends
3. **Honor Breaks**: Take full calculated breaks
4. **Adjust**: Find what works for your tasks
5. **Be Honest**: Stop when truly losing focus

### During Work
- **Single Task**: Focus on one thing
- **Minimize Distractions**: Remove temptations
- **Notice Signals**: Recognize when focus fades
- **Don't Force**: Stop when quality drops
- **Log Session**: Note what was accomplished

### During Breaks
- **Physical Movement**: Stand, walk, stretch
- **No Work**: Truly disconnect
- **No Screens**: Rest your eyes and mind
- **Hydrate**: Water, healthy snacks
- **Mindfulness**: Be present in break

## Scientific Basis

### Flow State Research
Based on Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi's work:
- Flow requires uninterrupted focus
- Takes 15-30 minutes to achieve
- Breaking flow is costly
- Quality work happens in flow

### Attention Research
- Focus capacity varies by individual
- Different tasks require different durations
- Natural attention fade signals are reliable
- Forced breaks can be counterproductive

### Recovery Science
- Break length should match effort expended
- Micro-breaks insufficient for long sessions
- Physical movement aids mental recovery
- Screen-free breaks more restorative
