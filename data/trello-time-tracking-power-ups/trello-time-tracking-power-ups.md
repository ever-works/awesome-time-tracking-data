## Overview

Trello does not include native time tracking features, focusing instead on visual task management through its kanban board interface. However, the Trello Power-Up ecosystem provides numerous time tracking integrations that add timers, estimates, and reporting directly to Trello cards.

## Popular Time Tracking Power-Ups

**Everhour** - Comprehensive time tracking:
- Embedded timer in Trello cards
- Time estimates on cards
- Budget tracking per board
- Detailed time reports
- Billable hours tracking
- Invoice generation
- Team capacity planning
- Browser extension for one-click tracking

**Harvest** - Professional time and expense tracking:
- Track time on Trello cards
- Generate invoices from tracked time
- Expense tracking integration
- Client billing and reporting
- QuickBooks integration
- Simple timer interface
- Project budget monitoring

**Clockify** - Free time tracking:
- Unlimited users on free tier
- Start/stop timer on cards
- Manual time entry
- Basic reporting
- Track billable vs non-billable
- Project and tag organization
- Desktop and mobile app sync

**Pomello** - Pomodoro-focused tracking:
- Pomodoro timer for Trello cards
- Track time in 25-minute intervals
- Break reminders
- Task completion tracking
- Focus time metrics
- Productivity analytics

**Plus for Trello** - Multiple time features:
- Time tracking on cards
- Card aging and metrics
- Recurring cards with time tracking
- Custom fields for time data
- Advanced reports
- Automation capabilities

**Toggl Track** - Simple time tracking:
- One-click time tracking from Trello
- Sync Trello cards to Toggl projects
- Detailed time reports
- Team time visibility
- Mobile app integration

## How Power-Ups Work

**Installation**:
1. Open Trello board
2. Access Power-Ups menu
3. Search for time tracking Power-Up
4. Add to board
5. Authorize and configure
6. Connect your account with the time tracking service

**Usage**:
- Timer button appears on Trello cards
- Click to start/stop tracking time
- Add manual time entries
- View time logged per card, list, or board
- Access reports in Power-Up or external tool

**Data Sync**:
- Time tracked in Trello syncs to external tool
- Full reporting happens in the time tracking app
- Some Power-Ups show summaries in Trello
- Changes to cards reflect in time tracking records

## Limitations

**Requires External Account** - Most Power-Ups need:
- Separate subscription to time tracking service
- Account creation and setup
- Additional cost beyond Trello

**Feature Limitations** - Compared to dedicated tools:
- Reporting more basic than standalone apps
- Advanced features require leaving Trello
- Integration can be less seamless than native features

**Per-Board Installation** - Power-Ups install per board:
- Must add to each relevant board
- Consistent setup across boards
- May need board admin permissions

## Pricing Considerations

**Trello**:
- Free tier available
- Standard: $5/user/month
- Premium: $10/user/month
- Power-Ups available at all tiers

**Time Tracking Services** (typical):
- Clockify: Free to $12/user/month
- Toggl Track: Free to $20/user/month
- Everhour: $8.50/user/month
- Harvest: $10.80/user/month

**Total Cost**: Trello + Time Tracking = $13-31/user/month

## Best Practices

**Choose the Right Power-Up**:
- **Everhour** - For teams wanting embedded Trello experience
- **Harvest** - For client billing and invoicing
- **Clockify** - For budget-conscious teams
- **Pomello** - For Pomodoro technique users
- **Toggl Track** - For simplicity and ease

**Implementation Tips**:
- Start with free tier to test workflow
- Train team on Power-Up usage
- Establish time tracking expectations
- Review reports regularly
- Integrate with billing processes

## When Trello Time Tracking Works Well

- Teams already using Trello for project management
- Visual task management is priority
- Simple time tracking needs
- Freelancers tracking client work
- Small teams with straightforward projects

- Complex project hierarchies
