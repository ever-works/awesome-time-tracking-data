## Overview

Getting Things Done (GTD) is a personal productivity system developed by David Allen and published in 2001. GTD alleviates overwhelm and instills focus, clarity, and confidence by moving all items of interest, relevant information, issues, tasks and projects out of one's mind and into an external, actionable system.

## Core Philosophy

The GTD method rests on a simple truth: the more information you have bouncing around inside your head, the harder it is to decide what to do first. Our brains are much better at processing information than storing it.

## The Five Steps

### 1. Capture
Capture anything that crosses your mind—nothing is too big or small! Every task, idea, or commitment goes into an inbox.

### 2. Clarify
Determine what items are actionable. For each item, decide: Is it actionable? If yes, what's the next action? If no, trash it, incubate it, or file it as reference.

### 3. Organize
Use your answers from the Clarify step to place items in the correct lists within your GTD system (Next Actions, Projects, Waiting For, Someday/Maybe, etc.).

### 4. Reflect
David Allen has called the weekly review a "critical factor for success." Regularly review all your lists to stay current and maintain perspective.

### 5. Engage
Execute on tasks with clarity, using your organized system to make trusted choices about what to do next.

## The Two-Minute Rule

If an action will take less than two minutes, do it immediately rather than adding it to your system.

## Impact

First published in 2001, with a revised edition in 2015, GTD has helped more than 3 million people bring clarity, focus, and ease to their work and life over three decades.
