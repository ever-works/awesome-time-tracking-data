## Overview

The Sunsama Daily Planning Ritual is a guided 10-15 minute workflow designed to help knowledge workers start each day with clarity and intention by deliberately choosing and planning their work.

## The Three Major Steps

### 1. Picking Out Things to Work On

- Review your calendar for upcoming meetings and commitments
- Pull in tasks from connected tools (Asana, Jira, Trello, etc.)
- Add any new tasks or priorities for the day
- Consider emails or messages requiring action

### 2. Deferring Non-Essential Work

- Identify tasks that don't need to happen today
- Move lower-priority items to future days
- Protect against over-commitment
- Create realistic, achievable daily scope

### 3. Ordering Your Work

- Arrange tasks in priority sequence
- Assign time estimates to each task
- Optionally timebox tasks onto your calendar
- Commit to your daily intention

## Key Principles

### Mindful Over Automated

**Philosophy**: "The planning ritual is the point."

Unlike Motion or FlowSavvy which automate scheduling, Sunsama emphasizes that taking 10 minutes each morning to deliberately choose your work creates focus and intentionality that an AI algorithm cannot replicate.

### Realistic Planning

- Encourages users to plan 5-6 hours of work for an 8-hour day
- Accounts for meetings, interruptions, and buffer time
- Prevents the common pitfall of over-scheduling
- Reduces stress from unrealistic expectations

### Pulled vs. Pushed Planning

- Users "pull" work from various sources into today
- Nothing is automatically assigned to your day
- You maintain complete control and agency
- Reduces feeling of being overwhelmed

## Automation Options

While Sunsama emphasizes manual intention, it offers:

**Automated Daily Planning Trigger**:
- Enable in Settings > Rituals
- Set a specific time to be automatically entered into planning
- System prompts you at your chosen time
- Still requires manual completion of ritual

**Integration Shortcuts**:
- Quick-add from connected tools
- Keyboard shortcuts for faster workflow
- Templates for recurring planning patterns

## Time Investment

**Daily Ritual**: 10-15 minutes
- 5-10 minutes for most users once proficient
- May take longer initially while learning
- Investment pays off in daily clarity and reduced decision fatigue

**Weekly Planning**: Optional additional ritual
- Review upcoming week
- Set weekly priorities
- Schedule larger blocks of work

## Comparison to Other Approaches

**Sunsama**: Manual, mindful, ritual-based
**Motion**: Fully automated AI scheduling
**FlowSavvy**: Semi-automated with manual triggers
**Traditional To-Do Lists**: Unstructured task lists

## Common Challenges

**Time Commitment**: Some users resist spending 10-15 minutes planning
- Counter: This investment prevents hours of distracted work

**Rigid Ritual**: Plans often change during the day
- Counter: Sunsama supports easy re-planning and adjustment

**Manual Overhead**: Requires daily discipline
- Counter: Can enable auto-prompt at set time for consistency
