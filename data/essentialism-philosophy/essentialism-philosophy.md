## Core Principle

The disciplined pursuit of less. Only once you give yourself permission to stop trying to do it all can you make your highest contribution.

## Key Tenets

- Less but better
- Disciplined pursuit of less
- Systematic approach to determining where our highest contribution lies
- Elimination of everything else

## The Essentialist Way

### Explore

Discern the vital few from the trivial many.

### Eliminate

Cut out non-essentials.

### Execute

Make execution effortless.

## Core Questions

- "If I didn't already own this, would I buy it?"
- "If I didn't have this opportunity, how much would I sacrifice for it?"
- "What is essential?"

## Practices

- Say no to non-essentials
- Set boundaries
- Protect time for what matters
- Build buffer into schedule
- Remove obstacles to essential activities
- Celebrate small wins

## Benefits

- Greater clarity
- More control
- Better results with less effort
- Reduced stress
- Increased satisfaction

## Trade-off Thinking

Essentialists accept that you can't have it all. Every yes is a no to something else.
