## Overview

Capacity planning in Float is the ability to see real-time team availability, spot scheduling conflicts, and balance workloads across your team from within the Schedule. This lets you allocate work realistically, avoid burnout, and make resourcing decisions that protect delivery timelines and margins.

## Key Features

### Real-Time Availability Visibility
Float provides instant visibility into:
- Current team capacity and utilization
- Available hours per team member
- Upcoming time off and holidays
- Existing project commitments
- Scheduling conflicts and over-capacity warnings

### Utilization Indicators
See live utilization indicators directly on the Schedule showing:
- Under-capacity team members (available for more work)
- Optimal capacity allocation
- Over-capacity situations requiring attention
- Projected future capacity based on current schedules

### Conflict Detection
Automatically identify:
- Double-booked team members
- Over-allocated resources
- Scheduling overlaps
- Capacity constraints
- Unrealistic workloads

### Workload Balancing
Float helps distribute work evenly:
- Visual indicators of team member workload
- Easy drag-and-drop task reassignment
- Skills-based allocation recommendations
- Workload distribution across projects

## How Capacity Planning Works

### 1. Resource Allocation
Allocate work by hours or percentages directly on Float's visual Schedule.

### 2. Availability Tracking
The system tracks:
- Pre-booked time off
- Existing project commitments
- Available capacity
- Holiday schedules

### 3. Automatic Warnings
Receive alerts for:
- Team members approaching or exceeding capacity
- Conflicting assignments
- Unrealistic deadlines
- Resource shortages

### 4. Adjustments
Easily adjust allocations with drag-and-drop:
- Shift tasks between team members
- Adjust time allocations
- Reschedule work to match capacity
- Balance workloads across the team

## Benefits

### Realistic Planning
Make allocation decisions based on actual availability rather than assumptions, preventing over-commitment and missed deadlines.

### Burnout Prevention
Visibility into workload distribution helps prevent team member burnout by catching over-allocation before it becomes problematic.

### Protected Margins
Proper capacity planning ensures projects stay on schedule and within budget, protecting profit margins.

### Proactive Resource Management
Spot resource shortages or surpluses in advance, allowing time to hire, train, or reallocate before problems arise.

### Improved Delivery
Realistic schedules based on actual capacity lead to more reliable delivery timelines and fewer last-minute scrambles.

This data continuously improves capacity planning accuracy.
