## Overview

Monday Hour One is a weekly planning practice where you dedicate the first hour of every Monday morning to planning and scheduling your entire week in advance using time blocking.

## The Concept

Every Monday at the start of your workday:
1. Review your goals and priorities
2. Identify the week's most important outcomes
3. Time block your entire week on your calendar
4. Schedule deep work before meetings claim the time
5. Include personal priorities and self-care

## Why Monday

**Fresh Start Energy**
Monday morning brings renewed energy and perspective

**Week-Long Impact**
Decisions made Monday morning affect entire week

**Proactive vs. Reactive**
Plan before others' requests fill your calendar

**Sets the Tone**
Intentional Monday creates intentional week

## The Monday Hour One Process

### Step 1: Brain Dump (10 minutes)
- List everything you think you need to do this week
- Include work projects, personal tasks, appointments
- Don't filter or prioritize yet

### Step 2: Prioritize (10 minutes)
- Identify top 3-5 priorities for the week
- Ask: "If I could only accomplish 3 things this week, what would they be?"
- Consider both urgent and important items
- Include one personal priority

### Step 3: Time Block (30 minutes)
- Open your calendar
- Block time for your top priorities FIRST
- Add already-scheduled meetings
- Block time for email, calls, admin work
- Include breaks, meals, personal time
- Add buffer blocks for flexibility

### Step 4: Review and Adjust (10 minutes)
- Does the calendar reflect your priorities?
- Is it realistic given your energy and commitments?
- Are there any conflicts or overcommitments?
- Make final adjustments

## Key Principles

### Principle 1: Schedule Priorities First
Don't fit priorities around meetings
Fit meetings around priorities

### Principle 2: Include Everything
Work, personal, self-care, breaks
A complete picture prevents overcommitment

### Principle 3: Be Realistic
Account for actual task durations
Include transition time
Don't schedule 100% of available time

### Principle 4: Plan for Flexibility
Schedule buffer blocks
Leave some time unscheduled
Expect and accommodate the unexpected

### Principle 5: Protect the Hour
Treat Monday Hour One as unmovable meeting
Decline other Monday morning commitments
This hour saves 5+ hours during the week

## Common Challenges

**"My week is too unpredictable"**
Block time but stay flexible
Adjust blocks as week unfolds
Some plan is better than no plan

**"Monday morning is too busy"**
Do Monday Hour One Friday afternoon
Or Sunday evening
The day matters less than the practice

**"I can't predict what will come up"**
You're right - so block buffer time
Leave 20-30% unscheduled
Having priorities clear helps you respond to unexpected

**"I don't have control over my calendar"**
Start with what you can control
Block before-work and after-work time
Negotiate meeting-free blocks with manager

## Daily Maintenance

After Monday Hour One:
- Each evening: Review tomorrow's blocks
- Each morning: Confirm today's plan
- Throughout day: Adjust as needed
- Friday: Review week, prep for next Monday Hour One

## Advanced Practices

### Theme Weeks
Alternate focus areas week-to-week:
- Growth week (new initiatives)
- Execution week (delivery)
- Maintenance week (cleanup)

### Quarterly Planning
Begin each quarter with extended planning:
- Monday Hour Three for quarterly review
- Set quarterly priorities
- Map to weekly Monday Hour One sessions

### Team Monday Hour One
Team spends first hour Monday planning together:
- Review team goals
- Coordinate week priorities
- Identify collaboration needs
- Align on key meetings

## Integration with Other Methods

**With Time Blocking:**
Monday Hour One IS time blocking at weekly level

**With GTD:**
Use GTD to capture and clarify
Use Monday Hour One to schedule execution

**With Eisenhower Matrix:**
Prioritize with matrix
Schedule Q1 and Q2 items in Monday Hour One

**With Deep Work:**
Schedule deep work blocks first in Monday Hour One

## Measuring Success

After 4 weeks, assess:
- Are top priorities getting completed?
- Does week feel more intentional?
- Is stress reduced?
- Are unexpected demands better managed?
- Is work-life balance improved?

## Use Cases

Monday Hour One is essential for:
- Executives with packed calendars
- Entrepreneurs with many priorities
- Parents balancing work and family
- Anyone whose week often feels reactive
- People struggling with work-life balance
- Managers coordinating team priorities

## The Philosophy

Monday Hour One embodies the principle: "If you don't plan your week, someone else will plan it for you."

By taking one hour every Monday to plan intentionally, you ensure that your week reflects YOUR priorities, not just everyone else's urgencies.

This single hour of planning saves hours of firefighting, reduces stress, and ensures that what matters most to you actually happens.
