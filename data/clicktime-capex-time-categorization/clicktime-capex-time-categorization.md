## The CapEx Challenge

Many organizations struggle to accurately track time spent on capital projects vs. operational expenses. This distinction matters for:
- **Financial Reporting**: GAAP and IFRS compliance
- **R&D Tax Credits**: Claiming government incentives
- **Budgeting**: Understanding true project costs
- **Capitalization**: Determining what can be capitalized on balance sheet

## How ClickTime Handles It

### Time Entry Categorization
When logging time, users can categorize work as:
- **Billable**: Client-facing work to be invoiced
- **Non-Billable**: Internal work, overhead
- **CapEx**: Capital expenditure (software development, IP creation)
- **R&D**: Research and development for tax credits

This categorization happens at the point of entry, ensuring accuracy.

## Why This Matters for Tech Companies

### Software Development as CapEx
Under accounting rules, certain software development can be capitalized:
- Application development stage
- Preliminary project stages (sometimes)
- Post-implementation stages (usually expensed)

ClickTime makes it easy to categorize development time correctly.

### R&D Tax Credits
Companies can claim tax credits for qualified research:
- Must track who worked on qualifying activities
- Document time spent on R&D
- Separate R&D from routine work
- Maintain contemporaneous records
