## Overview

Clockwise was an AI-powered calendar optimization platform that automatically rearranged meetings and protected focus time to help individuals, teams, and organizations reclaim valuable hours for deep work. The platform's AI models ran up to one million calendar permutations per team per day to create the best possible schedule for everyone.

## Important Update

The Clockwise team joined Salesforce in early 2026. Customer support was available until March 27, 2026. Clockwise partnered with Reclaim.ai to provide a smooth transition for users looking for AI-powered scheduling and calendar optimization.

## Key Features

### Flexible Meetings
When users marked internal recurring meetings as "flexible," Clockwise could move them to times that work better for all attendees. This feature only worked with internal attendees from the same organization.

### Focus Time Creation
Clockwise optimized for 2+ hour blocks of Focus Time when possible, but scheduled 60-90 minute blocks when weeks filled up. The system automatically protected time for deep, uninterrupted work.

### AI Assistant (Prism)
Users could interact with Clockwise via Prism, an intuitive chat + drag-and-click interface that provided shortcuts for scheduling and automated calendar management tasks.

### Calendar Integration
Featured seamless integration with Google Calendar and Microsoft Outlook, syncing across all user calendars and team members.

### Optimization Schedule
Flexible meetings optimization ran once daily at 4:00 PM in the organization's time zone, with a 20-hour protection window preventing last-minute changes.

## How It Worked

### AI Optimization Process
1. Analyzed team calendars and meeting patterns
2. Identified flexible meetings that could be rescheduled
3. Ran millions of permutations to find optimal arrangements
4. Automatically rescheduled meetings to create contiguous focus blocks
5. Balanced individual preferences with team coordination needs

### Focus Time Protection
The system prioritized creating uninterrupted blocks of time by:
- Identifying gaps in schedules
- Moving flexible meetings away from potential focus periods
- Coordinating across team members to align focus time
- Protecting mornings or afternoons based on user preferences

## Limitations

### Organizational Boundaries
Flexible meetings only worked with internal attendees from the same organization. External meetings remained fixed.

### Optimization Timing
Optimization ran once daily (4:00 PM organizational time), not continuously throughout the day.

### Protection Window
A 20-hour protection window prevented last-minute changes, meaning meetings within 20 hours couldn't be automatically rescheduled.

## Legacy and Transition
