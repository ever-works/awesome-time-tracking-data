## Overview

The Cognitive Engagement Research Institute conducted a significant study in 2026 examining the effectiveness of analog versus digital productivity tools.

## Key Findings

The research found that users who maintained physical planners completed 22% more tasks on average compared to those relying solely on digital tools. This finding provides empirical evidence for the continued value of analog productivity methods in an increasingly digital world.

## Implications

This research has influenced many professionals to adopt hybrid approaches to time management, combining digital tools with traditional paper-based planning systems like bullet journals and Moleskine planners.

## Methodology

The study tracked task completion rates across both analog and digital tool users over an extended period, accounting for various factors such as profession, work environment, and task complexity.
