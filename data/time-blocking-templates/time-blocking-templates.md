## Overview

Time blocking templates provide pre-designed daily and weekly schedule frameworks that help individuals and teams quickly adopt time blocking practices by offering proven patterns tailored to different roles, work types, and personal energy rhythms.

## Popular Template Types

### Maker Schedule
- **Pattern**: Long uninterrupted blocks
- **Design**: 3-4 hour deep work sessions
- **Breaks**: Substantial (20-30 min)
- **Meetings**: Batched at edges of day
- **Best for**: Developers, writers, designers, researchers

### Manager Schedule
- **Pattern**: Hourly slots
- **Design**: Back-to-back meetings possible
- **Flexibility**: Quick task insertion
- **Communication**: Throughout day
- **Best for**: Executives, team leads, client-facing roles

### Hybrid Schedule
- **Pattern**: Protected AM deep work
- **Design**: 9am-12pm focus, 1-5pm meetings/collab
- **Balance**: Creation and coordination
- **Flexibility**: Moderate
- **Best for**: Senior ICs, tech leads, consultants

## Building Your Template

### Step 1: Audit Current Time
- Track 1-2 weeks
- Note energy patterns
- Identify time wasters
- Find productive periods

### Step 2: Define Block Categories
- Deep focus work
- Collaborative work
- Administrative tasks
- Meetings
- Breaks and meals
- Personal time

### Step 3: Map to Calendar
- Place deep work at peak energy
- Batch similar activities
- Include transition time
- Protect breaks
- Mark boundaries

### Step 4: Test & Refine
- Try for 2 weeks
- Note what works/doesn't
- Adjust incrementally
- Stabilize before next change

## Implementation Tips

### Start Simple
- Begin with 3-4 block types
- Don't over-schedule initially
- Leave 30% buffer time
- Build complexity gradually

### Communicate Boundaries
- Share calendar publicly
- Set auto-responders
- Use status indicators
- Educate stakeholders

### Build Habits
- Same schedule for 2+ weeks
- Cue-based routines
- Environmental triggers
- Ritual reinforcement

### Review Regularly
- Weekly: What worked?
- Monthly: Adjust template
- Quarterly: Major revision
- Annually: Complete redesign

## Common Template Mistakes

### Over-Scheduling
- Every minute planned
- No buffer time
- Unrealistic expectations
- **Solution**: 60-70% capacity max

### Ignoring Energy
- Hard tasks when tired
- Creative work post-lunch
- Meetings during peak hours
- **Solution**: Energy-task matching

### No Flexibility
- Rigid adherence
- Can't handle unexpected
- Stressful when disrupted
- **Solution**: Buffer blocks, adapt not abandon
