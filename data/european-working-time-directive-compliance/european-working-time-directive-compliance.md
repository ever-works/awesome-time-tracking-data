## Overview

The European Working Time Directive establishes minimum requirements for working time, rest periods, and annual leave across EU member states, creating compliance requirements that drive time tracking adoption.

## Key Requirements

### Maximum Working Hours
- 48-hour maximum average working week
- Calculated over reference period (typically 17 weeks)
- Includes overtime
- Opt-out possible in some countries

### Rest Periods
- Minimum 11 consecutive hours rest per 24-hour period
- Minimum 24-hour rest period per week
- Regular breaks during working day

### Annual Leave
- Minimum 4 weeks paid annual leave
- Cannot be replaced by payment (except upon termination)
- Accrues during sick leave and maternity leave

## Time Tracking Requirements

To demonstrate compliance, organizations must:
- Track all working hours
- Record rest periods
- Document break times
- Monitor weekly/monthly totals
- Maintain records for inspection
- Generate compliance reports

## Enforcement

Member states enforce through:
- Labor inspections
- Penalties for non-compliance
- Worker complaints
- Trade union oversight
- Court rulings
