## Overview

Many companies are moving toward skills-based hiring as a strategic response to evolving labor market dynamics in 2026, according to SHRM research on the future of work.

## Impact on Time Tracking

Skills-based hiring changes how organizations think about productivity and time management:

- **Capability-Based Task Assignment**: Matching tasks to specific skills rather than job titles
- **Skill Development Time**: Tracking time invested in learning and skill acquisition
- **Cross-Functional Work**: Employees working across traditional departmental boundaries
- **Performance Metrics**: Measuring output based on skills applied rather than hours worked

## Benefits

- Expands talent pools by focusing on capabilities rather than credentials
- Enables more flexible workforce planning
- Allows better matching of skills to project needs
- Supports internal mobility and career development

## Time Management Implications

Organizations adopting skills-based approaches need time tracking systems that can:
- Track time by skill category, not just project or department
- Identify skill gaps and training needs
- Measure skill utilization and development
- Support dynamic team formation based on available skills

## 2026 Context

This trend reflects the broader shift toward outcomes-based work and away from traditional role-based organizational structures.
