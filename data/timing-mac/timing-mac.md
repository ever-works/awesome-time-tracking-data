## Overview

Timing is a Mac-native automatic time tracking app built with developers and creative professionals in mind, running in the background to automatically record your work without manual intervention.

## How It Works

Timing automatically records:
- **Applications used**: Which apps you're working in
- **Documents opened**: Specific files and their names
- **Webpages visited**: Sites you access for work
- **Code file names**: Even tracks specific code files
- **Time spent**: Duration in each activity

All data is stored locally on your device and visible only to you.

## Key Features for Developers

### Automatic Detection
- **IDE tracking**: Monitors VS Code, Xcode, IntelliJ, etc.
- **File-level detail**: Knows which files you edited
- **Git integration**: Connects activity to repositories
- **Stack Overflow tracking**: Records research time
- **Documentation time**: Tracks time reading docs

### Privacy-First Design
- **No screenshots**: Never captures your screen
- **Local storage**: All data stays on your Mac
- **You control data**: Export or delete anytime
- **No surveillance**: Designed for personal productivity, not monitoring

### Smart Categorization
- **Automatic rules**: Learn and categorize activities
- **Project assignment**: Intelligently map work to projects
- **Billable marking**: Flag billable vs. non-billable time
- **Customizable**: Adjust categorization rules as needed

## Comparison to Other Tools

### vs. RescueTime
- **Timing**: Mac-specific, more detailed, local storage
- **RescueTime**: Cross-platform, productivity analytics focus

### vs. Toggl Track
- **Timing**: Fully automatic, no manual timers
- **Toggl**: Manual tracking, cross-platform

### vs. Clockify
- **Timing**: Premium Mac app, automatic
- **Clockify**: Free, manual, web-based
