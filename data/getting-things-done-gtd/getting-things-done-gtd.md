## Overview

Getting Things Done (GTD) is a comprehensive personal productivity system developed by David Allen that rests on moving all tasks and commitments out of your mind and into an external, trusted system.

## Core Principle

"There is an inverse relationship between things on your mind and those things getting done." Your mind is for having ideas, not holding them.

## The Five-Step Workflow

**1. Capture**
Collect everything that has your attention into collection buckets (inbox, notebook, app)

**2. Clarify**
Process what each item means and what to do about it
- Is it actionable?
- If yes: What's the next action?
- If no: Trash, incubate, or reference

**3. Organize**
Put items into appropriate categories:
- Projects list
- Next actions list
- Waiting for list
- Someday/maybe list
- Calendar
- Reference materials

**4. Reflect**
Review your system regularly:
- Daily: Review calendar and next actions
- Weekly: Complete weekly review of all lists

**5. Engage**
Choose what to do based on context, time available, energy, and priority

## Key Rules

**The 2-Minute Rule:**
If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately rather than capturing it.

**Weekly Review:**
The most critical habit - review all lists, projects, and commitments weekly.

## Benefits

- Clear mind and reduced stress
- Nothing falls through cracks
- Confidence in commitments
- Better focus on current task
- Appropriate engagement with all areas of life

## Common Pitfalls

Skipping the weekly review is the #1 reason GTD implementations fail. Without regular reviews, the system becomes unreliable.
