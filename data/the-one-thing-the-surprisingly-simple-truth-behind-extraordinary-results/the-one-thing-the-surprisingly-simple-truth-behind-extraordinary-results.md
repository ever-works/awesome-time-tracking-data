## Overview

The One Thing by Gary Keller and Jay Papasan challenges the conventional wisdom of multitasking and doing more. The central thesis is that extraordinary results are achieved not by doing more things but by identifying and focusing on the single most important action in any area of life.

## Core Concept

- **The Focusing Question**: "What's the ONE thing I can do such that by doing it everything else will be easier or unnecessary?"
- This question can be applied from the broadest life goals down to immediate daily actions

## Key Insights

- Multitasking is a myth — shifting focus between tasks creates inefficiency and errors
- Willpower is a finite resource; use it on what matters most
- The domino effect: a small, well-chosen action can trigger a chain reaction of increasingly larger results
- Success is sequential, not simultaneous — build on one thing at a time

## Six Lies Between You and Success

- Everything matters equally
- Multitasking
- A disciplined life
- Willpower is always on will-call
- A balanced life
- Big is bad

## The Path to Greatness

- Identify your domain (purpose, priority, productivity)
- Ask the focusing question within each domain
- Time-block your ONE thing and protect that time
