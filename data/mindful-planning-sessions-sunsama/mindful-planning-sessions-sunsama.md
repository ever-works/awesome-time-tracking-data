## Philosophy Behind Mindful Planning

Sunsama emphasizes that every day should feel calm and focused, rejecting the traditional productivity approach of cramming maximum tasks into minimum time.

## The Planning Ritual

### Step 1: Review
- Look at yesterday's accomplishments
- Note uncompleted tasks
- Reflect on what worked and what didn't

### Step 2: Import
- Pull tasks from connected tools (Asana, Trello, Jira, etc.)
- Review calendar commitments
- Identify what truly needs attention today

### Step 3: Prioritize
- Select realistic number of tasks
- Consciously choose based on impact
- Consider energy levels and capacity

### Step 4: Time-Block
- Assign tasks to specific calendar times
- Estimate realistic durations
- Build in buffer time
- Visualize how day will flow

### Step 5: Set Intention
- Define what success looks like today
- Choose one most important outcome
- Commit to the plan

## Key Principles

### Realistic Over Optimistic
- Plan for 4-6 hours of focused work, not 8-10
- Account for meetings, breaks, and interruptions
- Quality over quantity of tasks

### Intentional Selection
- Actively choose what matters most
- Say no (or not today) to less important items
- Focus on meaningful progress

### Calendar Integration
- Tasks become time blocks
- See work in context of day
- Identify capacity limits visually

## Impact on Burnout Prevention

- Prevents chronic overcommitment
- Builds sustainable work habits
- Creates realistic expectations
- Reduces guilt about incomplete lists
