## Overview

Capacity planning uses time tracking data to understand team availability, forecast resource needs, and allocate work efficiently while preventing overallocation and burnout.

## Key Metrics

### Available Capacity
- Total work hours available
- Minus: PTO, holidays, meetings
- Minus: Non-billable time
- Result: Hours available for project work

### Utilization Rate
- Billable hours / Total available hours
- Target varies by role and industry
- Monitors productivity and allocation

### Allocation
- Hours committed to projects
- Current vs. future allocation
- Identifies over/underallocation

## Process

### 1. Calculate Available Capacity
- Work hours per week (typically 40)
- Subtract average meeting time
- Subtract average admin time
- Account for PTO and holidays
- Result: Billable capacity per person

### 2. Track Current Allocation
- Hours assigned to active projects
- Time spent on each project
- Remaining project hours
- Forecast completion dates

### 3. Forecast Future Needs
- Pipeline projects and estimates
- Required skills and roles
- Timeline constraints
- Capacity gaps

### 4. Make Allocation Decisions
- Assign resources to projects
- Balance workload across team
- Identify hiring needs
- Decide what work to decline/delay

## Best Practices

### Don't Over-Allocate
- Plan for 70-85% utilization, not 100%
- Leave buffer for urgent work
- Account for estimation errors
- Prevent burnout

### Use Historical Data
- Average time per project type
- Typical utilization by role
- Seasonal patterns
- Individual performance data

### Update Regularly
- Weekly capacity reviews
- Adjust for schedule changes
- Track actual vs. planned
- Reforecast based on progress

### Skills-Based Planning
- Match skills to tasks
- Identify skill gaps
- Cross-training opportunities
- Specialist vs. generalist allocation
