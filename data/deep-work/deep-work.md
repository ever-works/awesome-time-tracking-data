## Overview

Deep Work, a concept developed by Cal Newport, refers to professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration. These efforts push your cognitive capabilities to their limit, create new value, improve your skill, and are hard to replicate.

## Deep Work vs Shallow Work

**Deep Work:**
- Cognitively demanding tasks
- Requires full concentration
- Creates significant value
- Difficult to replicate
- Examples: writing, coding, strategic analysis

**Shallow Work:**
- Non-cognitively demanding tasks
- Often performed while distracted
- Doesn't create much value
- Easy to replicate
- Examples: email, scheduling, admin tasks

## The Deep Work Hypothesis

The ability to perform deep work is becoming increasingly rare and valuable in our economy. Those who cultivate this skill will thrive.

## Deep Work Strategies

1. **Rhythmic** - Deep work same time daily
2. **Bimodal** - Alternate between deep work periods and other work
3. **Monastic** - Minimize shallow obligations entirely
4. **Journalistic** - Fit deep work wherever you can

## Rules for Deep Work

- Work deeply: ritualize deep work sessions
- Embrace boredom: train concentration ability
- Quit social media: be selective with distractions
- Drain the shallows: minimize shallow work

## Benefits

- Higher quality output
- Faster skill development
- Greater career value
- Increased productivity
- More meaningful work experience
