## Overview

Zapier Time Tracking Automation Workflows are pre-built integrations (called "Zaps") that automatically record, sync, and consolidate time entries across calendars, project management tools, and accounting systems. These automations eliminate manual data entry, ensure every billable minute is captured, and keep teams instantly informed of time tracking changes.

## How It Works

A Zap is a connection between two apps, made of a trigger and an action. Whenever the trigger event happens, Zapier automatically executes the action event. For time tracking, this means activities in one app (like starting a calendar event) automatically trigger time entries in your time tracking tool.

## Common Workflow Patterns

### Calendar-Based Time Tracking
- **Trigger**: Calendar event starts
- **Action**: Automatically start time tracker
- **Benefit**: Never forget to track time for meetings or scheduled work

### Invoice Automation
- **Trigger**: New time entry logged
- **Action**: Add to invoice in accounting software
- **Benefit**: Streamlined billing without manual timesheet review

### Project Management Sync
- **Trigger**: Task created or updated in project tool
- **Action**: Create corresponding time tracking entry
- **Benefit**: Keep time tracking and project status synchronized

### Timesheet Reporting
- **Trigger**: End of week/month
- **Action**: Compile time entries into spreadsheet report
- **Benefit**: Automated reporting for review and analysis

Connect a Pomodoro timer with your time tracking app so Zapier handles one of the timers for you automatically.

Automatically start and stop time tracking based on calendar events, ensuring all meeting time is captured.

### Multi-Tool Consolidation
