## Overview

Time blocking is a productivity method where you divide your day into blocks of time, each dedicated to accomplishing specific tasks or types of work. Rather than working from an open-ended to-do list, you assign every task to a specific time slot in your calendar.

## Core Concept

Turn your calendar into a detailed daily roadmap by:
- Scheduling specific time blocks for different activities
- Assigning concrete start and end times to tasks
- Protecting blocks from interruptions
- Working on only the scheduled activity during each block

## How to Time Block

1. **List Your Tasks**: Identify all tasks and projects
2. **Estimate Duration**: Determine how long each will take
3. **Check Your Calendar**: Review existing commitments
4. **Create Blocks**: Assign tasks to specific time slots
5. **Add Buffer Time**: Include breaks and transition time
6. **Review & Adjust**: Adapt as needed throughout the day

## Types of Time Blocks

- **Deep Work Blocks**: 90-120 minutes for focused, complex work
- **Shallow Work Blocks**: 30-60 minutes for administrative tasks
- **Meeting Blocks**: Scheduled meetings and calls
- **Email/Communication Blocks**: Dedicated time for email and messages
- **Break Blocks**: Rest, lunch, and recovery time
- **Buffer Blocks**: Cushion between activities for transitions
- **Personal Blocks**: Exercise, family time, hobbies

## Best Practices

- **Theme Your Days**: Assign different types of work to different days (Marketing Monday, Development Tuesday)
- **Batch Similar Tasks**: Group similar activities together
- **Protect Your Blocks**: Treat time blocks as non-negotiable appointments
- **Use the 80/20 Rule**: Block 80% of time, leave 20% flexible for unexpected items
- **Block Your Best Hours**: Schedule deep work during peak energy times
- **25/50-Minute Meetings**: End meetings 5-10 minutes early for transitions
- **Color Code**: Use calendar colors to visualize different work types

## Benefits

- **Eliminates Decision Fatigue**: Pre-decided work schedule
- **Increases Focus**: Single-tasking instead of multitasking
- **Improves Time Estimation**: Learn how long tasks actually take
- **Prevents Overcommitment**: Visual representation of available time
- **Reduces Procrastination**: Specific start times create urgency
- **Work-Life Balance**: Blocks for personal time ensure it happens
- **Reduces Context Switching**: Batching similar tasks

## Common Challenges

- **Interruptions**: Unexpected urgent items disrupt blocks
- **Overestimation**: Tasks take longer than blocked time
- **Rigidity**: Feeling constrained by the schedule
- **Planning Time**: Creating blocks takes time itself

## Solutions

- Include buffer blocks for unexpected items
- Track actual time vs estimated to improve accuracy
- Allow flexibility - blocks are guidelines, not prison
- Use time blocking tools/apps to automate scheduling

## Combining with Other Methods

- **With GTD**: Time block your Next Actions list
- **With Pomodoro**: Use 25-minute pomodoros within blocks
- **With Eisenhower Matrix**: Block time for Q2 (important but not urgent) activities
- **With Goals/OKRs**: Schedule blocks for key result activities

## Best For

- Knowledge workers with control over schedule
- People who struggle with procrastination
- Those wanting better work-life boundaries
- Anyone feeling scattered or unfocused
- Remote workers managing their own time

## Not Ideal For

- Highly reactive jobs (customer service, emergency response)
- Roles with unpredictable schedules
- People who feel constrained by strict schedules

## Getting Started

1. Start with one week of tracking how you currently spend time
2. Identify your most productive hours
3. Begin by blocking 2-3 deep work sessions
4. Add blocks for recurring activities (email, meetings)
5. Review and adjust daily
6. Gradually increase blocking coverage to 80% of day
