## What is the Zeigarnik Effect?

The Zeigarnik Effect is a psychological phenomenon describing our tendency to remember uncompleted tasks better than completed tasks. Named after Soviet psychologist Bluma Zeigarnik.

## How It Affects Productivity

### Negative Impacts
- Unfinished tasks create mental clutter
- Incomplete work causes intrusive thoughts
- Can lead to stress and anxiety

### Positive Applications
- Can motivate task completion
- Creates natural drive to finish what you start
- Can be used strategically to maintain momentum

## Leveraging the Effect

- Stop work before full completion to make resuming easier
- Create "ready-to-resume" plans before switching tasks
- Break large projects into completable sub-tasks
- Use end-of-day shutdown rituals

## Related Concepts

Attention residue, task switching costs, flow state, cognitive load theory.
