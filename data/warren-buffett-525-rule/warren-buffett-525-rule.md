## Overview

The 5/25 Rule (also known as the "2 List Strategy") is a prioritization method attributed to Warren Buffett that forces ruthless focus on what matters most by actively avoiding distractions.

## The Process

**Step 1: Write Down 25 Goals**
- Career goals
- Personal goals
- Skills to develop
- Projects to complete
- Anything you want to accomplish

**Step 2: Circle Your Top 5**
- Review the entire list
- Identify the 5 most important
- Circle only those 5
- Be honest and selective

**Step 3: Create Two Lists**
- **List A:** The 5 you circled (Focus List)
- **List B:** The 20 you didn't circle (Avoid-At-All-Costs List)

**Step 4: The Critical Insight**
List B isn't "get to these when you have time." These are things to AVOID until your top 5 are completed. They're distractions, no matter how appealing.

## Why It Works

**The Trap of "Pretty Good" Goals:**
- Items 6-20 are attractive enough to get your attention
- But not important enough to deserve your focus
- They steal energy from your top priorities
- They create the illusion of progress

**Ruthless Prioritization:**
- Forces difficult choices
- Eliminates "good but not great"
- Creates real focus
- Prevents dilution of effort

## Key Insights

**Avoid vs. Defer:**
Most people treat their avoid list as "someday/maybe." Buffett says actively avoid them—they're your primary distraction.

**Enemy of Great:**
"Good" is the enemy of "great." Your 6-20 items are good goals that prevent you from achieving great things in your top 5.

**Energy is Limited:**
You have limited time, energy, and focus. Every yes to item #15 is a no to items #1-5.

## Application Areas

**Career Focus:**
- List 25 career goals
- Identify 5 most important
- Avoid the other 20 distractions

**Skills Development:**
- List 25 skills to learn
- Focus on mastering 5
- Ignore the other 20

**Projects:**
- List 25 project ideas
- Commit to 5
- Put 20 on hold indefinitely

## Implementation Tips

- Do this exercise annually
- Be brutally honest with rankings
- Don't cheat by having "5.5" goals
- Review your avoid list regularly
- Say no to avoid-list opportunities
- Only add to focus list when one is complete
- Share your focus list for accountability

## Benefits

- Extreme clarity on priorities
- Reduced decision fatigue
- Deeper work on important goals
- Faster progress on top priorities
- Less guilt about what you're not doing
- Better results from focused effort
