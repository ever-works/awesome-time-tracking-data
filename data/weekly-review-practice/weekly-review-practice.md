## Overview

The Weekly Review is a cornerstone habit of the Getting Things Done (GTD) system, providing a regular checkpoint to get clear, current, and creative about all your commitments.

## Why It Matters

The weekly review is the most common reason GTD fails - when skipped, your trusted system becomes outdated and unreliable, and you stop trusting it. This creates a downward spiral.

## The Weekly Review Process

**1. Get Clear (30-60 minutes)**
- Collect loose papers and materials
- Process your inboxes to zero
- Empty your head - capture new items
- Review previous calendar week
- Review upcoming calendar (next 2-3 weeks)

**2. Get Current**
- Review all project lists
- Review next actions lists
- Review waiting for list
- Review someday/maybe list
- Update project plans

**3. Get Creative**
- Review goals and areas of responsibility
- Identify new projects
- Capture any new ideas
- Look for improvement opportunities

## Best Practices

**Schedule It**: Treat it as a non-negotiable appointment
**Same Time/Place**: Create a consistent ritual
**No Interruptions**: Protect this time
**Complete It**: Don't skip steps
**Make It Pleasant**: Create an enjoyable environment

## Benefits

- Regain control and perspective
- Rebuild trust in your system
- Prevent items from falling through cracks
- Reduce stress and anxiety
- Enable confident decision-making
- Foster creativity and strategic thinking

## Time Required

- Beginners: 2-3 hours initially
- Experienced: 1-2 hours
- Well-maintained system: 30-60 minutes

## Common Timing

- Friday afternoon (prep for next week)
- Sunday evening (start week fresh)
- Monday morning (set weekly intentions)
