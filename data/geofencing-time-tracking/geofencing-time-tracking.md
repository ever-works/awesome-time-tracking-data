## Overview

Geofencing time tracking uses GPS technology to create virtual boundaries around job sites or work locations. When employees with mobile devices enter or exit these boundaries, the system automatically records their arrival and departure times.

## How It Works

1. **Geofence Setup**: Managers define virtual perimeters around work locations using GPS coordinates
2. **Automatic Detection**: Employee mobile devices detect when crossing geofence boundaries
3. **Auto Clock-In/Out**: System automatically starts or stops time tracking based on location
4. **Real-Time Updates**: Managers receive live notifications of employee arrivals and departures

## Key Benefits

- **Eliminates Manual Clock-Ins**: No need for employees to remember to start timers
- **Prevents Time Theft**: Employees must physically be at the location to be clocked in
- **Verifies Location**: GPS coordinates prove where employees worked
- **Simplifies Field Tracking**: Ideal for construction, service, sales, and field teams
- **Reduces Administrative Work**: Automatic tracking eliminates timesheet reconciliation

## Common Use Cases

- Construction job sites with multiple locations
- Field service technicians traveling between customer sites
- Sales representatives visiting client locations
- Healthcare workers providing home-based care
- Delivery drivers and logistics personnel

## Privacy Considerations

Modern geofencing systems only track location when employees are clocked in or during work hours, respecting privacy by not monitoring after hours.

## Technology Requirements

- GPS-enabled smartphones or tablets
- Mobile time tracking app with geofencing capability
- Cellular or WiFi connectivity for real-time sync
- Battery optimization to prevent excessive drain
