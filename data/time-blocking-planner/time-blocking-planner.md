## Overview

Time blocking planners are specialized tools (physical notebooks or digital apps) designed specifically for the time blocking methodology. They provide structured layouts with hourly grids that help visualize and plan how each hour of the day will be used.

## Features

### Physical Planners
- Hourly time grids (typically 5 AM - 9 PM)
- Daily, weekly, and monthly views
- Space for priorities and notes
- Habit tracking sections
- Reflection prompts

### Digital Planners
- Drag-and-drop time block creation
- Calendar integration
- Color coding by category
- Recurring block templates
- Analytics on time allocation

## Popular Options

- **Cal Newport's Time Block Planner**: Physical planner from the Deep Work author
- **Digital calendars**: Google Calendar, Outlook with blocking
- **Specialized apps**: Reclaim.ai, Motion, Structured
- **Notion templates**: Custom time blocking setups

## Time Blocking Process

1. **Morning Planning**: Review day and assign tasks to blocks
2. **Block Creation**: Schedule specific time for each priority
3. **Execution**: Follow the plan, noting deviations
4. **Evening Review**: Reflect on what worked and adjust

## Benefits

- Visual representation of time allocation
- Intentional scheduling reduces decision fatigue
- Protected time for important work
- Realistic view of available time
- Better work-life boundaries

## Best Practices

- Block time the night before or early morning
- Include buffer blocks for flexibility
- Schedule breaks and transitions
- Color code by activity type
- Review and adjust weekly

## Integration with Methods

- **Deep Work**: Block uninterrupted focus time
- **Pomodoro**: Divide blocks into pomodoros
- **Time Chunking**: Structure blocks in 30-minute chunks
- **Day Theming**: Assign themes to full-day blocks

## Use Cases

- Executives managing complex schedules
- Remote workers structuring their days
- Students balancing classes and study
- Anyone struggling with time management
- People seeking work-life balance
