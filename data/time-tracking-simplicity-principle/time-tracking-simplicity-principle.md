## Overview

The Simplicity Principle in time tracking states that the easier the tracking process, the more accurate and complete the data will be. Complex systems with excessive detail requirements often produce lower quality data due to user resistance and stress.

## Key Principles

- Minimize required fields and categories
- Don't expect too many details in timesheets
- Reduce friction in time entry process
- Make tracking as automatic as possible
- Balance detail needs with user burden
- Focus on essential data only
- Significant effort creates stress leading to poor data quality

## Benefits

- Higher adoption rates
- More complete time data
- Better accuracy
- Reduced employee frustration
- Lower administrative overhead
- Sustainable long-term usage
- Better data quality overall

## Implementation

Implement simplicity by starting with minimal requirements, adding complexity only when proven necessary, using smart defaults, enabling quick entry methods, and regularly removing unused fields or categories.
