## Overview

Interstitial journaling is a productivity and mindfulness practice that involves taking brief notes between tasks throughout the day, combining time awareness with reflective note-taking.

## How It Works

### Core Practice
- Before switching tasks, pause and make a brief journal entry
- Note the current time and what you just completed
- Record what you plan to do next
- Include 1-2 sentences about your current state or observations

### Implementation in Tools
- Roam Research: Use /time command to insert timestamps
- Notion: Create timestamped entries in daily notes
- Paper journal: Write time manually alongside task notes
- Digital tools: Any note-taking app with timestamp support

## Benefits

### Time Awareness
- Creates natural awareness of time passage
- Documents actual time spent on activities
- Reveals patterns in work habits
- Highlights frequent context switches

### Mindfulness
- Builds intentionality between tasks
- Creates micro-breaks for reflection
- Reduces autopilot behavior
- Increases presence and focus

### Documentation
- Automatic work log creation
- Context for later reference
- Record of decision-making process
- Timeline of project evolution

## Best Practices

- Keep entries brief (30 seconds each)
- Focus on observation, not perfection
- Note energy levels and mood
- Review weekly for patterns
- Don't let it become a burden
