## Overview

MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive) is a principle for organizing time tracking categories that ensures data accuracy and prevents reporting errors. In time tracking, MECE means no two categories overlap and every hour of work has a clear home.

## Core Principles

### Mutually Exclusive
- Each time entry can only belong to one category
- No overlapping definitions between categories
- Clear boundaries prevent double-counting

### Collectively Exhaustive
- Every possible work activity fits into at least one category
- No gaps in the categorization system
- Complete coverage of all billable and non-billable time

## Benefits

- Foundation for trustworthy reports and analytics
- Eliminates categorization confusion for team members
- Ensures accurate billing and budget tracking
- Enables meaningful cross-project comparisons
- Reduces data entry errors and rework

## Implementation Guidelines

- Aim for five to ten top-level categories maximum
- Audit structure quarterly to remove unused categories
- Document clear definitions for each category
- Test with team members before full rollout
- Use sub-categories for granular tracking within main categories

## Common MECE Violations to Avoid

- Having both "Client Work" and "Billable Work" as categories (overlap)
- Categories like "Miscellaneous" that are too broad (not exclusive)
- Missing categories for common activities like training or admin (not exhaustive)
