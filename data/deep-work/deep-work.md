## Overview

Deep Work is a concept and methodology developed by Cal Newport in his book "Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World." It defines professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push cognitive capabilities to their limit.

## The Four Rules

1. **Work Deeply**: Create routines and rituals to support deep work sessions
2. **Embrace Boredom**: Train your mind to resist distraction
3. **Quit Social Media**: Eliminate or drastically reduce shallow digital distractions
4. **Drain the Shallows**: Minimize low-value activities

## Deep Work Philosophies

- **Monastic**: Eliminate or drastically minimize shallow obligations
- **Bimodal**: Divide time between deep work periods and everything else
- **Rhythmic**: Create regular deep work routine with scheduled blocks
- **Journalistic**: Quickly switch between deep and shallow work as needed

## Benefits

- Enables quick learning of complicated new skills
- Produces high-value output at high rate
- Creates competitive advantage in knowledge economy
- Improves focus and cognitive performance
- Generates flow states more reliably

## Implementation

Newport recommends starting with small Deep Work sessions (nothing over 90 minutes) and gradually building focus stamina over time.
