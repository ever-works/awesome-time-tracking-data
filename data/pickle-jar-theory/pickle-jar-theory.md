## The Metaphor

Your time is a pickle jar filled with:

- **Rocks**: Biggest priorities (major projects, key relationships)
- **Pebbles**: Secondary priorities (smaller projects, obligations)
- **Sand**: Minor tasks (emails, calls, errands)
- **Water**: Filler activities (social media, TV, browsing)

## Key Insight

If you put sand and water in first, there's no room for rocks. But if you place rocks first, sand and water fill the gaps.

## Application

### Put Rocks First

1. Identify 3-5 most important priorities
2. Schedule dedicated time for these first
3. Protect this time
4. Build week/day around these priorities

### Then Add Pebbles, Sand, Water

Schedule secondary tasks around rocks, handle minor tasks in gaps, filler activities take remaining time.

## Common Mistakes

- Starting with sand (email in morning)
- No defined rocks
- Treating pebbles as rocks
- Forgetting water (some downtime necessary)

## Practical Implementation

- Each Sunday, identify rocks for week
- Block time for rocks first
- Schedule pebbles around rocks
- Leave gaps for sand and water
