## Overview

Profit First is a business cashflow management system created by Michael Michalowicz. It fundamentally changes how businesses handle their finances by using a profit-first allocation model rather than the traditional formula of Sales - Expenses = Profit. Instead, it operates on Sales - Profit = Expenses, ensuring that profit is taken first and the business operates within the remaining funds.

## Core Principles

- **Profit First Formula**: Revenue - Profit = Expenses (reverses the conventional formula)
- **Multiple Bank Accounts**: Allocates income into separate accounts for Profit, Owner's Pay, Tax, and Operating Expenses
- **Periodic Distribution**: Regular allocation percentages applied to deposits to maintain financial discipline
- **Reality-Based Cash Management**: Works with actual cash flow rather than accrual accounting
- **Progressive Allocation**: Percentages increase over time as the business adapts to leaner operating budgets

## Key Features

- Structured bank account system to physically separate different financial purposes
- Percentage-based allocation that adjusts as revenue grows
- Forced frugality by constraining available operating expenses
- Regular profitability check-ins through scheduled distributions
- Clear visibility into true business financial health

## Use Cases

- Small and medium businesses seeking to ensure consistent profitability
- Business owners who struggle with cashflow management and end-of-year surprises
- Organizations looking to build disciplined financial habits and reserves
- Entrepreneurs wanting to reverse the pattern of being busy but not profitable
