## Overview

The Action Method is a productivity system developed by Behance founder Scott Belsky, designed to help individuals and teams manage tasks and projects more effectively. Scott Belsky introduced the action method in his book "Making Ideas Happen," where he aimed to assist creative professionals in overcoming inefficiency and disorganization.

## Core Components

The action method encourages individuals to categorize tasks into three distinct categories:

### 1. Action Steps
Tasks that need immediate attention - these are specific, concrete tasks that move a project forward. These are the actionable items that require your focus now.

### 2. Backburner Items
Tasks or ideas that are not immediately actionable but may be important in the future. These are kept in a separate list to revisit later without cluttering your immediate action items.

### 3. Reference Materials
Notes and materials related to the project - supporting resources that may be useful later. This includes documentation, research, and any information that supports your work.

## How to Get Started

1. Capture all actionable tasks using a notebook or digital tool
2. Categorize tasks into Action Steps, Backburner Items, and Reference Materials
3. Assign priority levels to Action Steps
4. Schedule regular reviews to update and refine action items

## Benefits

- Simplifies task management by categorizing actionable items into distinct groups
- Provides clarity on priorities and next steps
- Helps individuals maintain focus on current priorities without overlooking important responsibilities
- Shifts focus from idea generation to execution
- Makes it easier to break down complex projects into manageable, actionable steps

## Ideal Users

Particularly useful for creative professionals who want to move from ideation to execution and need a system that helps them organize thoughts, tasks, and resources effectively.
