## Overview

Zoe Read-Bivens is a teacher and productivity expert who created the Flowtime Technique (also known as Flowmodoro) in 2016. Her innovation addressed limitations she observed in the traditional Pomodoro Technique, offering a more flexible approach to time management.

## The Flowtime Technique

Read-Bivens developed Flowtime to overcome shortcomings of rigid, timer-based productivity systems. Her key insight: instead of forced 25-minute intervals, workers should be allowed to maintain focus for as long as concentration naturally lasts, then take proportional breaks.

## Core Innovation

The technique's revolutionary aspect is its flexibility:
- **No Fixed Intervals**: Workers continue focused work until concentration naturally wanes
- **Proportional Breaks**: Rest periods are calculated as approximately 20% of the work session duration
- **Respects Flow States**: Allows deep work to continue uninterrupted rather than breaking it artificially
- **Counts Up, Not Down**: Unlike Pomodoro's countdown timers, Flowtime counts up to track session length

## Impact on Productivity

Read-Bivens' contribution has been particularly valuable for:
- Creative professionals who enter extended flow states
- Knowledge workers doing complex problem-solving
- Anyone who finds rigid time blocks disruptive
- Tasks requiring sustained, uninterrupted concentration

## Philosophy

Her approach represents a shift from external time management (following predetermined schedules) to internal time management (working with natural attention spans and energy rhythms). This philosophy aligns with the growing recognition that productivity is personal and should adapt to individual working styles.

## Legacy

Since 2016, the Flowtime Technique has gained adoption among productivity enthusiasts seeking alternatives to traditional time boxing methods. It represents an important evolution in time management thinking, emphasizing flexibility and individual differences over one-size-fits-all solutions.
