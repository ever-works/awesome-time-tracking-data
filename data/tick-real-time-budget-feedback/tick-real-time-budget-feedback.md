## Overview

Tick's real-time budget feedback is a core differentiator that updates project and task budgets instantly as team members submit time entries. This immediate visibility helps teams stay profitable by identifying budget issues as they develop rather than discovering overruns after the fact.

## How It Works

1. **Set Budgets**: Assign hour budgets to projects and individual tasks
2. **Track Time**: Team members log time against projects/tasks
3. **Instant Updates**: Budget remaining updates immediately upon time entry submission
4. **Visual Indicators**: Color-coded warnings show budget health
5. **Alerts**: Notifications when budgets approach or exceed limits

## Real-Time Visibility

**Dashboard Display**:
- See all project budgets at a glance
- Color coding indicates budget status (green/yellow/red)
- Percentage consumed shown for each project/task
- Trending indicators show rate of consumption

**Individual Task Budgets**:
- Break projects into smaller budgeted tasks
- Track against granular budgets
- Identify specific trouble spots
- Reallocate hours between tasks as needed

## Benefits

**Prevents Overruns**:
- Catch budget issues immediately
- Make adjustments before significant over-budget occurs
- Alert team to slow down or stop work if needed

**Improves Profitability**:
- Stay within quoted budgets
- Maintain project margins
- Avoid delivering work at a loss

**Better Planning**:
- Historical budget data improves estimates
- Learn which tasks consume more time than expected
- Refine future project budgeting

**Team Awareness**:
- Everyone sees budget status
- Self-regulation of time usage
- Shared responsibility for profitability

## Competitive Advantage

Many time trackers:
- Show budgets only in reports
- Require manual calculation
- Provide delayed feedback
- Don't make budget status visible to team

Tick's real-time approach means budget management is proactive rather than reactive, giving teams the best opportunity to remain profitable by surfacing issues immediately when they can still be addressed.
