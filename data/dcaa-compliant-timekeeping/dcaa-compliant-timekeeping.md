## Overview

DCAA (Defense Contract Audit Agency) compliance refers to meeting U.S. government standards for time tracking and billing on federal contracts. Contractors must maintain rigorous time records to support billing.

## Core Requirements

### Daily Recording
- Time recorded daily
- No retrospective time entry
- Same-day or next-day recording
- Contemporary documentation

### Detail Level
- Specific tasks and projects
- Job/contract numbers
- Work descriptions
- Location information

### Approval Process
- Supervisor review and approval
- Electronic signature trails
- Approval timing requirements
- Change documentation

### Audit Trail
- All changes logged
- Original entries preserved
- Modification reasons documented
- Complete history maintained

## Supporting Documentation

Required elements:
- Employee signatures
- Supervisor approvals
- Charge number validation
- Time period totals
- Supporting work records

## Software Requirements

DCAA-compliant systems must:
- Prevent backdating
- Require daily entry
- Lock approved periods
- Maintain complete audit trails
- Support multiple approval levels
- Generate DCAA-format reports

## Common Deficiencies

Audits frequently find:
- Retroactive time entry
- Inadequate approval documentation
- Missing audit trails
- Insufficient task detail
- Incomplete records

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

Consequences include:
- Questioned costs
- Payment withholding
- Contract suspension
- Loss of future contracts
- Legal action
- Reputation damage

## Best Practices

### Process Design
