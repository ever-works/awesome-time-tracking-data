## Overview

The Flowtime Technique was created by Zoe Read-Bivens as a flexible alternative to the Pomodoro Technique, allowing users to maintain focus for as long as natural energy permits.

## How It Works

- Work on a single task without fixed time requirements
- Continue working as long as focus remains strong
- Take breaks when natural fatigue sets in
- Track actual work duration for future planning
- Discourage multitasking in favor of solo, focused work

## Connection to Ultradian Rhythms

The technique aligns with ultradian rhythms—natural 90-120 minute cycles of alertness and fatigue throughout the day. Most people can sustain deep focus for around 90 minutes before the body craves rest and recovery.

## Key Advantages

- Not bound by fixed timer requirements
- Allows entering and maintaining flow states
- Provides data on actual time allocation
- Helps predict future task duration
- Respects individual energy patterns
- More flexible than rigid time-blocking methods

## Productivity Impact

One technology company that directed employees to take breaks every hour saw productivity levels rise by 23% with no mandate required.
