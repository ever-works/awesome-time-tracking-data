## Overview

Sprint velocity measures the amount of work (typically story points) a team completes in a sprint, building predictability through historical averages for accurate planning.

## Calculation

**Velocity = Sum of story points for completed user stories in sprint**

**Average Velocity = Mean over last 3-7 sprints**

## Benefits

- Predictable capacity estimation
- Release date forecasting  
- Team performance trends
- Realistic sprint planning
- Continuous improvement metrics

## Best Practices

1. Track 5-7 sprints for reliable average
2. Use consistent story point scale
3. Don't compare teams (different contexts)
4. Focus on trend not absolute numbers
5. Account for team changes and holidays

## Common Pitfalls

- Using as performance metric
- Expecting constant increases
- Ignoring context changes
- Gaming the numbers
- Cross-team comparisons
