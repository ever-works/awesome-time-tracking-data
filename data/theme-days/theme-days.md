## Overview

Theme Days (also called Day Theming) is a productivity method where you assign each day of the week a specific theme, focus area, or type of work. This creates structure, reduces context switching, and helps you make progress on different priorities throughout the week.

## How to Implement

1. Identify your major work categories or focus areas
2. Assign each category to a specific day
3. Schedule related tasks and meetings on theme days
4. Protect theme days from unrelated work when possible
5. Review and adjust themes quarterly

## Example Theme Day Schedules

**For Executives/Managers**:
- Monday: Strategic Planning & Team Meetings
- Tuesday: Client/Customer Focus
- Wednesday: Internal Operations & Admin
- Thursday: Product/Project Development
- Friday: Learning & Professional Development

**For Creative Professionals**:
- Monday: Client Meetings & Communication
- Tuesday: Deep Creative Work
- Wednesday: Collaboration & Brainstorming
- Thursday: Production & Execution
- Friday: Admin, Planning & Skill Development

**For Entrepreneurs**:
- Monday: Marketing & Business Development
- Tuesday: Product Development
- Wednesday: Client Delivery
- Thursday: Operations & Finance
- Friday: Strategy & Learning

## Benefits

- Dramatically reduces context switching
- Easier to batch similar tasks and meetings
- Provides weekly structure and rhythm
- Makes it easier to say no to off-theme requests
- Improves deep work opportunities
- Helps balance competing priorities
- Simplifies weekly planning

## Best Practices

- Start with broad themes, refine over time
- Maintain some flexibility for urgent items
- Communicate themes to team to help with scheduling
- Use morning for theme-specific deep work
- Review theme effectiveness monthly
- Adjust themes based on business priorities
